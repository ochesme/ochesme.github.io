Answer:

The simplest organic compounds are those composed of only two elements: carbon and hydrogen. These compounds are called hydrocarbons. Hydrocarbons themselves are separated into two types: aliphatic hydrocarbons and aromatic hydrocarbons. Aliphatic hydrocarbons are hydrocarbons based on chains of C atoms. There are three types of aliphatic hydrocarbons. Alkanes are aliphatic hydrocarbons with only single covalent bonds. Alkenes are aliphatic hydrocarbons that contain at least one C–C double bond, and alkynes are aliphatic hydrocarbons that contain a C–C triple bond. Occasionally, we find an aliphatic hydrocarbon with a ring of C atoms; these hydrocarbons are called cycloalkanes (or cycloalkenes or cycloalkynes).

Aromatic hydrocarbons, such as benzene,are flat-ring systems that contain continuously overlapping p orbitals.Electrons in the benzene ring have special energetic properties that give benzene physical and chemical properties that are markedly different from alkanes. Originally, the term aromatic was used to describe this class of compounds because they were particularly fragrant. However, in modern chemistry the term aromatic denotes the presence of a very stable ring that imparts different and unique properties to a molecule.

The simplest alkanes have their C atoms bonded in a straight chain; these are called normal alkanes. They are named according to the number of C atoms in the chain. The smallest alkane is methane:

10. List down some of the basic and industrial uses of hydrocarbon compounds ​


Answer:

Hydrocarbon compounds have a wide range of uses in both basic and industrial settings. Some common uses of hydrocarbons include:

Basic Uses:

Fuel for transportation, heating, and cooking

Lighting and fuel for lamps and lanterns

Solvents for cleaning and degreasing

Lubricants and oils for machinery

Raw materials for the production of plastics and synthetic materials

Natural gas used for cooking, heating, and electricity generation

Propane and butane used for fuel in portable stoves and camping equipment

Kerosene used for lighting, heating, and as a fuel for jet engines

Industrial Uses:

Raw materials for the production of chemicals such as plastics, pharmaceuticals, and fertilizers

Petroleum products used for fuel, such as gasoline, diesel, and aviation fuel

Petrochemicals used in the production of plastics, synthetic fibers, and rubber

Hydrocarbon waxes used in the production of candles, polishes, and cosmetics

Lubricants and greases used in machinery and equipment

Solvents used in paints, coatings, and adhesives

Fuel for industrial processes, such as refining and metallurgy

Raw materials for the production of carbon black used in the manufacture of tires and other rubber products.

Organic compounds are a large group of chemical compounds whose molecules contain carbon, except carbides, carbonates and carbon oxides. The study of organic compounds is called organic chemistry. Many of the organic compounds, such as proteins, fats, and carbohydrates, are important components of biochemistry. Among several classes of organic compounds are aliphatic compounds, carbon chains whose functional groups can be changed; aromatic hydrocarbons, compounds containing at least one benzene ring; heterocyclic compounds that include non-carbon atoms in their ring structure; and polymers, long chains of repeating groups.

The difference between organic and inorganic chemistry is the presence/absence of carbon-hydrogen bonds. Thus, carbonic acid is considered inorganic, while formic acid, the first fatty acid, is organic.m The name "organic" refers to its history, in the 19th century, where it was believed that organic compounds could only be made/synthesized in the body of organisms through the vis vitalis - life-force.
