12. why species of Garnet is similar physical properties and cristal form, but differ in chemical composition​


Explanation:

Garnets are a group of minerals that share similar crystal structures and physical properties, such as hardness, cleavage, and specific gravity. However, the chemical composition of garnets can vary significantly depending on the specific species.

Garnets are classified based on their chemical composition, which can include different combinations of elements such as aluminum, calcium, iron, magnesium, and manganese. These elements can substitute for each other in the crystal lattice structure of the garnet, leading to variations in the chemical composition and resulting in different species of garnet.

For example, the garnet species almandine and pyrope both have the same crystal structure and physical properties, but differ in their chemical composition. Almandine is a type of garnet that contains more iron than pyrope, while pyrope contains more magnesium than almandine. This difference in chemical composition can result in differences in color, with almandine typically being a darker red color and pyrope being a brighter red color.

In summary, while different species of garnet may have similar physical properties and crystal forms, their chemical compositions can vary significantly, leading to differences in their appearance and other characteristics.

20. Identify the Garnet rock​


Answer:

Garnet rock is a type of metamorphic rock that contains garnet minerals. Garnet is a group of silicate minerals that are commonly found in igneous, metamorphic, and sedimentary rocks. Garnet rocks are typically formed from the high-pressure, high-temperature metamorphism of sedimentary rocks, such as shale, sandstone, or limestone, that contain small amounts of garnet.

Garnet rocks are usually dark in color and have a crystalline texture. The color of the rock can range from brown to black, depending on the amount and type of garnet present. Garnet minerals are typically hard and durable, which makes garnet rocks ideal for use in construction and industrial applications, such as abrasives, cutting tools, and waterjet cutting.

Overall, garnet rocks are easily recognizable by their dark color and crystalline texture, as well as the presence of garnet minerals.
