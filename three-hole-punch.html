8. it is a long bamboo tube euth three finger holes, and is played by blowing thought a small hole with the nose.?​


Answer:

Nose flute

Explanation:

Nose flute

A nose flute is made from a slender bamboo tube.

A nose flute belongs to the aerophone family of the Hornbostel-Sach's classification because the production of sound is made through blowing a stream of air on the hole of the bamboo through the nose.  

A nose flute is called tongali by the Kalinga and Kalaleng by the Itneg, Bayo and Kankanaey. Usually around two feet in length a kalaleng has holes cut in the side, to be stopped by the fingers producing the notes. The player closes one nostril with a bit of cotton, then forces the air from the other into a small hole cut in the end of the tube.

The Cordillerans believe that the sound is more soothing to hear when it is produced through the nose of the player.

13. What force is required to punch a 25-mm-diameter hole in a plate that is30 mm thick? The shear strength is 450 MN/m2.​


Answer:

1060.29 KN

Step-by-step explanation:

Given:

τ = 450 MN/m^2 = 450 x 10^6 N/m^2

t = 30 mm = 0.03 m

d = 25 mm = 0.025 m

SOLUTION:

Compute the area

A = (π)dt

A = (π)(0.025)(0.03)

A = 2.356 x 10^-3

τ = F/A

F = Aτ

F = (2.356 x 10^-3)(450 x 10^6 N/m^2)

F = 1,060,287.52 N

or

F = 1060.29 KN

26. s e. 4) A cooler contains ten bottles of sports drink: four lemon-lime flavored, three orange flavored, and three fruit-punch flavored. Three times, you randomly grab a bottle, return the bottle to the cooler, and then mix up the bottles. The first time, you get a lemon-lime drink. The second and third times, you get fruit-punch.​


Answer:

Based on the given information, the probability of the events can be calculated as follows:

Let's denote the flavors as follows: LL - Lemon-lime flavored O - Orange flavored FP - Fruit-punch flavored

Given: Total number of bottles in the cooler = 10 Number of lemon-lime flavored bottles = 4 Number of orange flavored bottles = 3 Number of fruit-punch flavored bottles = 3

First event: Getting a lemon-lime flavored drink P(LL) = Number of lemon-lime flavored bottles / Total number of bottles P(LL) = 4/10 = 2/5

After the first event, the bottle is returned to the cooler and the bottles are mixed up again.

Second event: Getting a fruit-punch flavored drink P(FP) = Number of fruit-punch flavored bottles / Total number of bottles P(FP) = 3/10

After the second event, the bottle is returned to the cooler and the bottles are mixed up again.

Third event: Getting a fruit-punch flavored drink P(FP) = Number of fruit-punch flavored bottles / Total number of bottles P(FP) = 3/10

Since each event involves randomly grabbing a bottle with replacement (i.e., the bottle is returned to the cooler and mixed up again), the probabilities of each event are independent of each other.

Therefore, the probability of getting a lemon-lime flavored drink on the first event, and fruit-punch flavored drinks on the second and third events is: P(LL) * P(FP) * P(FP) = (2/5) * (3/10) * (3/10) = 18/250 = 0.072 or 7.2%.
