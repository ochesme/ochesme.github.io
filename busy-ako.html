Answer:

Business accounting is the process of recording, classifying, and summarizing financial transactions to provide information that is useful in making business decisions. This information is used by management, investors, creditors, and government agencies to evaluate a company's financial performance and to make decisions about the allocation of resources. Business accounting includes tasks such as creating financial statements, maintaining financial records, and preparing taxes. It is a vital function in any organization, as it helps to ensure the accuracy and integrity of financial information and enables the organization to make informed decisions.
