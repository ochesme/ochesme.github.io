What is the element,principles and processes applied or used in-Ritualistic votive statue (Sumerians)-Stele of hammurabi introduced the legal system (Babylonians)-Naturalistic Portrait of their leader (Akkadians)-Human-headed winged beasts stone carved at the gate of citadels to impress and to intimidate (Assyrians)-Decorative design for great ancient architecture (Neo Babylonians)NEED NA NEED KO NA PATULONG


Answer:

Explanation:

-Ritualistic votive statue (Sumerians)

Element: form (almost resembles a cylinder), color (ivory), texture ( clay, rough on the touch)

Principles: balance (assymetrical), proportion (shape of a woman)

Process: The votive statues are of various sizes and usually carved in gypsum or limestone. The statues are usually carved with the hands clasped, right over left, at the chest or waist in a gesture of attentiveness. Some figures hold cups or branches of vegetation.

-Style of Hammurabi introduced the legal system (Babylonians)

Element: lines, shape(flat), form (cube or square, tablestone), texture (text encrypted, embossed)

Principles: Rhythm, emphasis, harmony

Process: The Code of Hammurabi was one of the earliest and most complete written legal codes and was proclaimed by the Babylonian king Hammurabi.

-Naturalistic Portrait of their leader (Akkadians)

Element: Value (depending on lighting, it can be light or dark),  texture (embossed scales, ribbed)

Principles:  proportion (of the head), gradation

Process: The figure's hair has been elaborately braided so that it circles the head and ends in a knot at the back. The curled beard represents royalty and also popular fashion. Heated rods were often used to curl hair in the ancient world. The face was deliberately damaged (the eyes gauged out).

-Human-headed winged beasts stone carved at the gate of citadels to impress and to intimidate (Assyrians)

Element: balance, proportion (human head and animal body), harmony

Principles: shape, form, texture, color

Process: The sculptor gave these guardian figures five legs so that they appear to be standing firmly when viewed from the front but striding forward when seen from the side.  

-Decorative design for great ancient architecture (Neo Babylonians)

Element: lines (consistency of the pillars), shape, color (ivory and greens)

Principles: balance, rhythm (natural components), space

Process: Beginning with Nabopolassar's coronation as King of Babylon in 626 BC and being firmly established through the fall of the Neo-Assyrian Empire in 612 BC, the Neo-Babylonian Empire and its ruling Chaldean dynasty would be short-lived, being conquered after less than a century by the Persian Achaemenid Empire in 539 BC.
