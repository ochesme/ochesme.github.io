Answer:

Skewness is a measure of symmetry, or more precisely, the lack of symmetry. ... Kurtosis is a measure of whether the data are heavy-tailed or light-tailed relative to a normal distribution. That is, data sets with high kurtosis tend to have heavy tails, or outliers.

7. difference between normal curve distribution, positive skew and negative skew?​


Answer:

Negative skew refers to a larger or thicker tail on the left side of the distribution, whereas positive skew refers to a longer or fatter tail on the right side of the distribution. Positively skewed data will have a mean that is higher than the median.

Negative skew refers to a longer or fatter tail on the left side of the distribution, while positive skew refers to a longer or fatter tail on the right.
