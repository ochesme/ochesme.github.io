6. why should raw, high-risk food never be stored above cooked/ready to eat food?


Answer:

Raw, high-risk food should never be stored above cooked/ready to eat food because the bacteria from raw food can contaminate cooked food, and the bacteria can multiply to dangerous levels if the food is not cooked thoroughly again. Raw food should always be stored below ready-to-eat or cooked food in refrigerators and display cabinets. This way, juices from the raw food cannot drip onto cooked food. Cross-contamination happens when cooked or ready-to-eat food contacts raw food.

9. Cooking RiceJerome starts to cook rice. He starts to soak the rice at 7:30 pmBy what time will the rice be ready for serving? Answer: ​


Answer:

The time it takes to cook rice can vary depending on the type of rice and the cooking method. However, a common rule of thumb is that rice takes about 18-20 minutes to cook once it has been brought to a boil.

Assuming Jerome is using this common cooking method, if he starts soaking the rice at 7:30 pm, he will need to bring the rice to a boil and then cook it for 18-20 minutes. Let's say it takes him 5 minutes to bring the rice to a boil.

Then, the rice will be ready for serving at:

7:30 pm (start soaking) + 5 minutes (bring to a boil) + 18-20 minutes (cooking time) =

7:53-7:55 pm

Therefore, the rice will be ready for serving between 7:53 and 7:55 pm.
