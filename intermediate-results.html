8. similarities of felsic and intermediate​


Igneous Rocks by CompositionIgneous rocks are classified according to their mineral content: Ultramafic rocks are dominated by olivine and/or pyroxene. Mafic rocks are dominated by plagioclase and pyroxene smaller amounts of olivine. Intermediate rocks are roughly even mixtures of felsic minerals and mafic minerals. There is little or no quartz. Felsic rocks are mostly feldspar, at least 10% quartz, and less than 15% mafic minerals. Within these four groups, rocks are named depending on whether they display coarse-grained or fine-grained textures. The coarse-grained textures indicate intrusive rocks whereas the fine-grained textures generally indicate extrusive rocks. 30. What is the intermediate step in the form (x + a)² = b as a result of completing the square for the following equation?-6x² + 64x - 222 = -8x​


Given :-What is the intermediate step in the form (x + a)² = b as a result of completing the square for the following equation ?[tex]-6x² + 64x - 222 = -8x[/tex]To find :-What is the intermediate step ?

Solution :-

Given equation :-

[tex] \sf \qquad \to \boxed{ \bold{-6x^2+64x-222=-8x}}[/tex]

When completing the square, first move the terms in x to the left and the constant to the right of the equation :-

[tex] \sf\implies -6x^2+64x-222=-8x[/tex]

[tex] \sf\implies -6x^2+64x-222+222=-8x+222[/tex]

[tex] \sf\implies -6x^2+64x=-8x+222[/tex]

[tex] \sf\implies -6x^2+64x+8x=-8x+222+8x[/tex]

[tex] \sf\implies -6x^2+72x=222[/tex]

Factor out the leading coefficient -6 from the left side, then divide both sides by -6 :-

[tex] \sf\implies -6(x^2-12x)=222[/tex]

[tex] \sf\implies \dfrac{-6(x^2-12x)}{-6}=\dfrac{222}{-6}[/tex]

[tex] \sf\implies x^2-12x=-37[/tex]

Add the square of half the coefficient of the term in x to both sides, forming a perfect square trinomial on the left side :-

[tex] \sf\implies x^2-12x+\left(\dfrac{-12}{2}\right)^2=-37+\left(\dfrac{-12}{2}\right)^2[/tex]

[tex] \sf\implies x^2-12x+\left(-6\right)^2=-37+\left(-6\right)^2[/tex]

[tex] \sf\implies x^2-12x+36=-37+36[/tex]

[tex] \sf\implies x^2-12x+36=-1[/tex]

Factor the perfect square trinomial on the left side :-

[tex] \qquad \sf\implies (x-6)^2=-1[/tex]

To solve :-

[tex] \sf\implies \sqrt{(x-6)^2}=\sqrt{-1}[/tex]

[tex] \sf\implies x-6=\pm i[/tex]

[tex] \sf\implies x-6+6=\pm i+6[/tex]

[tex] \sf\implies x=6 \pm i[/tex]

Therefore, the solutions are :-

[tex] \qquad \qquad \sf \mapsto \: \boxed{\bold{ \: \: x=6+i, \quad x=6-i \: \: }}[/tex]
