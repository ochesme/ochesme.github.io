12. Why can water striders walk across the surface of the water? What can you possibly do to make these insects sink?


Answer:

They have hydrophobic legs and take advantage of the surface tension of water.

Explanation:

There are numerous reasons why water striders can walk across the surface of water, but the major reasons are because they have hydrophobic legs and they are able to take advantage of the surface tension of water.

Water has high surface tension. Meanwhile, the legs of a water strider are long and slender. This allows the weight of the water strider body to be distributed over a large surface area. Because their weight is evenly distributed the water molecules can accommodate this tension so that they wouldn't sink.

The legs of a water strider is also covered with hydrophobic substance. This repels water and is thus helping them stay afloat.
