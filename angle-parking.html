19. A pole stands in a park such that its shadow increases by 1 m when the angle of elevation of sun charges from 45° to 30°. Determine the height of the pole.​


TRIGONOMETRY

[tex]\normalsize{\blue{\overline{\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad}}}[/tex]

A pole stands in a park such that its shadow increases by 1 m when the angle of elevation of sun charges from 45° to 30°. Determine the height of the pole.​

[tex]\large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION}:}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{Let \: AO \: be \: the \: pole \: of \: height \: h:}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{tan \: 45^{\circ} = \dfrac{AO}{OB} }[/tex]

[tex]\sf{1 = \dfrac{h}{x} }[/tex]

[tex]\sf{h = x \: \: \: ... \: (1)}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{In \: \triangle AOC:}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{tan \: 30^{\circ} = \dfrac{AO}{OC} }[/tex]

[tex]\sf{\rightarrow \: \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{3} } = \dfrac{h}{x + 2} }[/tex]

[tex]\sf{\rightarrow \: x + 2 = \sqrt{3h} }[/tex]

[tex]\sf{\rightarrow \: \sqrt{3h} -x = 2}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{\rightarrow \: (\sqrt{3}-1)h=2}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{\rightarrow \: h = \dfrac{2}{\sqrt{3}-1 } }[/tex]

[tex]\sf{\rightarrow \: h = \dfrac{2}{\sqrt{3}-1 } \times \dfrac{\sqrt{3} +1}{\sqrt{3} +1} }[/tex]

[tex]\sf{\rightarrow \: h = \dfrac{2}{2}(\sqrt{3}+1) }[/tex]

[tex]\sf{\rightarrow \: h = (\sqrt{3}+1) }[/tex]

[tex]\sf{Therefore, \: height \: of \: the \: pole = (\sqrt{3}+1) m }[/tex]

23. A ramp in Parking garage rises to connect two levels of Parking space. The two levels are parallel with each other. The ramp makes 15 with the lower horizontal what angle does the ramp make with the upper horizontal?​


Answer:

Since the two levels of parking space are parallel, the ramp forms a right triangle with the lower horizontal and the hypotenuse being the ramp itself. The angle between the ramp and the lower horizontal is 15 degrees.

Let's call the angle between the ramp and the upper horizontal "x". Since the sum of the angles in a triangle is 180 degrees, we know that the third angle in the right triangle formed by the ramp, lower horizontal, and hypotenuse is 90 degrees. Therefore, we can use the fact that the sum of the angles in a triangle is 180 degrees to find x:

x + 90 + 15 = 180

Simplifying this equation gives:

x = 75

Therefore, the angle between the ramp and the upper horizontal is 75 degrees.

Answer: Carlo is 180 meters away from the canteen.

Step-by-step explanation:

Use the Pythagorean Theorem to find the length of the hypotenuse of ΔCAB. Let x be AB.

[tex]300^{2} + 400^{2} = x^{2} \\90,000 + 160,000 = x^{2} \\x^{2} = 250,000\\x= 500[/tex]

The length of AB is 500 meters. Use Corollary 2 of Geometric Mean Theorem to find the length of AD.

[tex]\frac{AB}{CA} = \frac{CA}{AD}[/tex]

Let x be AD.

[tex]\frac{500}{300} = \frac{300}{x} \\\\500x = 90,000\\x = 180[/tex]

The length of AD is 180 meters. Therefore, the distance between the Canteen and Carlo's spot is 180 meters.
