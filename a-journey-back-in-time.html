Step-by-step explanation:

When the car moves from A to B, it travels at an unknown speed, which we'll call "v1".

When the car moves from B to A, it travels at two different speeds - 50 km/hr and 30 km/hr. We can calculate the average speed for this portion of the journey using the formula for the average of two speeds:

average speed = (2 x speed1 x speed2) / (speed1 + speed2)

where speed1 and speed2 are the two speeds that the car travels at.

So the average speed of the car from B to A can be calculated as:

average speed = (2 x 50 km/hr x 30 km/hr) / (50 km/hr + 30 km/hr)

average speed = 3000 / 80

average speed = 37.5 km/hr

The total time taken for the journey is the sum of the time taken to travel from A to B and the time taken to travel from B to A.

Time taken to travel from A to B = d / v1

Time taken to travel from B to A = d / 37.5 km/hr

Total time taken = (d / v1) + (d / 37.5 km/hr)

The average speed of the car for the entire journey can be calculated by dividing the total distance traveled by the total time taken:

Average speed = (2d) / [(d / v1) + (d / 37.5 km/hr)]

Simplifying this expression by taking the common factor of "d" in the denominator, we get:

Average speed = (2d / d) / [(1 / v1) + (1 / 37.5 km/hr)]
