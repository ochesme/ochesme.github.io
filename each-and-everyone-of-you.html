Answer:

Haiku: "An old silent pond" by Matsuo Basho

An old silent pond

A frog jumps into the pond—

Splash! Silence again.

Interpretation: This haiku by Matsuo Basho describes an old, silent pond, which is suddenly disturbed by a frog jumping in and creating a splash. The poem highlights the power of silence and stillness, as well as the suddenness and unexpectedness of change. The theme of the poem is the transience of life and the impermanence of all things. Basho employs a number of poetic techniques in the poem, including juxtaposition (contrasting the quiet pond with the sudden splash) and onomatopoeia (the word "splash" imitates the sound of the frog jumping into the water).
