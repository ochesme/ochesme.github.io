8. 2. A box of chocolates contains 10 milk chocolates, 8 dark chocolates, and 6 white chocolates. Hanissa randomly chooses a chocolate, eats it, and then randomly chooses another chocolate. What is the probability that Hanissa chose a milk chocolate, and then, a white chocolate?


There are a total of 24 chocolates in the box.

The probability of choosing a milk chocolate on the first pick is 10/24.

After eating the first chocolate, there are 23 chocolates left, with 6 white chocolates remaining.

So the probability of choosing a white chocolate on the second pick, given that a milk chocolate was chosen first, is 6/23.

To find the probability of both events happening, we multiply the probabilities:

(10/24) x (6/23) = 15/92

Therefore, the probability that Hanissa chose a milk chocolate, and then a white chocolate is 15/92.

17. A box of chocolates contains 10 milk chocolates, 8 dark chocolates, and 6 white chocolates. Hanissa randomly chooses a chocolate, eats it, and then randomly chooses another chocolate. What is the probability that Hanissa chose a milk chocolate, and then, a white chocolate?​


Answer:

There are 24 chocolates in total, and Hanissa has to choose a milk chocolate first, and then a white chocolate. The probability of choosing a milk chocolate first is 10/24, since there are 10 milk chocolates out of 24 chocolates in total. After Hanissa eats the milk chocolate, there are 23 chocolates left in the box, and 6 of them are white chocolates. Therefore, the probability of choosing a white chocolate second is 6/23.

To find the probability of both events happening, we multiply the probability of choosing a milk chocolate first by the probability of choosing a white chocolate second, given that a milk chocolate was already chosen. This gives:

(10/24) x (6/23) = 0.0652 or approximately 0.07 (rounded to two decimal places)

Therefore, the probability that Hanissa chose a milk chocolate, and then a white chocolate is approximately 0.07 or 7%.

Find the amount of 12 pieces of candies.Find the amount of one chocolate bar.Find the amount paid by Rose.Find how many bars of chocolates and pieces of candies that could be bought with the amount paid by Rose.

What is the number sentence?

1. Find the amount of 12 pieces of candies.

We know that a piece of candy is Php 4. To find the amount of 12 pieces of candies, multiply pieces of candies by the amount of each.

12 × 4 = N

2. Find the amount of one chocolate bar.

Once we know the cost of candies, we need to subtract it from the amount of Mary paid and divide the number by 6, as the number of chocolate bars.

(Php 198 - cost of candies) ÷ 6 = N

3. Find the amount paid by Rose.

It is Php 100 less than.

Php 198 - Php 100 = N

4. Find how many bars of chocolates and pieces of candies that could be bought with Php 98.

List the multiples of each number.
