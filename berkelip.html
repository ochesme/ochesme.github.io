2. Explain Berkeley’s Idealism


Answer:

For Berkely, there is not a world with physical objects that is external to us. The sense data we perceive with our sense organs are bundled together as 'ideas'. For example the idea 'chair' is a bundle of sense data such as browness, rods and plank shapes etc. Unlike indirect realists, Berkeley does not think these ideas represent anything outside of our perception; objects only exist when someone is perceiving.

Answer:

Berkeley was an idealist. He held that ordinary objects are only collections of ideas, which are mind-dependent. Berkeley was an immaterialist. ... There is less agreement on Berkeley's argumentative approach to idealism and immaterialism and on the role of some of his specific arguments.


3. who is John Jacob Berzelius


Answer:
Jacob Berzelius was one of the founders of modern chemistry. He was the first person to measure accurate atomic weights for the elements, which helped to confirm Dalton's Atomic Theory and was the basis of Mendeleev's periodic table.

4. ANSWERwho in Jon's Jakob Berzelius?​


Answer:

Jöns Jacob Berzelius was a Swedish chemist who is considered one of the founders of modern chemistry. He made important contributions to the understanding of atomic structure, chemical reactions, and the nature of chemical bonding. Berzelius is known for his work on the development of the modern system of chemical symbols and the development of the concept of atomic weights. He is also credited with the discovery of several new elements, including cerium, selenium, and thorium. Berzelius was a pioneer in the field of chemistry and his work laid the foundations for many important advances in the field.

Some elements named after places.Write the elements name below?

1.CALIFORNIUM elements

2.BERKELIUM elements

3.EUROPIUM elements

Explanation:

1.Californium is a radioactive chemical element with the symbol Cf and atomic number 98. The element was first synthesized in 1950 at the Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory (then the University of California Radiation Laboratory), by bombarding curium with alpha particles (helium-4 ions).

2.Berkelium is a transuranic radioactive chemical element with the symbol Bk and atomic number 97. It is a member of the actinide and transuranium element series. It is named after the city of Berkeley, California, the location of the Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory where it was discovered in December 1949.

3.Europium is a chemical element with the symbol Eu and atomic number 63. Europium is the most reactive lanthanide by far, having to be stored under an inert fluid to protect it from atmospheric oxygen or moisture
