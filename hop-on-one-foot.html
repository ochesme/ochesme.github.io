19. 1. Movement of the feet described as a spring on one foot and landing on the same foot. ( hop,jump )pa help po


Answer:

The locomotor movement performed is called leaping.

Explanation:

A leap is performed by jumping from one foot and landing on the other. During this motion, the body is in between the push off and the landing. A leap can also be combined  during a walk or a run.  

Leaping is an example of an even rhythm category. Other examples include: walk, run, jump, hop and leap. But, do not be confused between leaping and hopping. Leaping is jumping on one foot and landing on the other while hopping is jumping on one foot and landing on the same foot.

On the other hand, locomotor movements in general are movements where the body travels from one space to another. While non-locomotor movements are movements that do not cause the body to travel to another space.
