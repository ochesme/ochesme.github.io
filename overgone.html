26. 3. Which of these experimental observations is correct for the force required to overcome static and kinetic friction? * A. The force required to overcome static friction is always zero. B. The force required to overcome static friction is less than the force required to overcome kinetic friction C. The force required to overcome static friction is equal to the force required to overcome kinetic friction D. The force required to overcome static friction is higher than the force required to overcome kinetic friction. ​


Answer:

A. The force required to overcome static friction is always zero.

Explanation:

The value of static friction varies between zero and the smallest force needed to start motion. This smallest force required to start motion, or to overcome static friction, is always greater than the force required to continue the motion, or to overcome kinetic friction.
