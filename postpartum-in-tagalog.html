1. overview of postpartum/puerperium?​


Answer:

The postpartum period, also known as the puerperium and the "fourth trimester," refers to the time after delivery when maternal physiologic changes related to pregnancy return to the nonpregnant state.


2. what is postpartum hemorrhage?


Answer:

Postpartum hemorrhage (also called PPH) is when a woman has heavy bleeding after giving birth. It's a serious but rare condition. It usually happens within 1 day of giving birth, but it can happen up to 12 weeks after having a baby.

10. difference between antepartum, intrapartum and postpartum??​


Answer:

As adjectives the difference between antepartum and intrapartum. is that antepartum is describing the period before childbirth; antenatal while intrapartum is during parturition (childbirth).

“Postpartum” means the time after childbirth. Most women get the “baby blues,” or feel sad or empty, within a few days of giving birth. For many women, the baby blues go away in 3 to 5 days. If your baby blues don't go away or you feel sad, hopeless, or empty for longer than 2 weeks, you may have postpartum depression.

Postpartum depression is a type of serious depression that occurs within four weeks following birth, according to the DSM-5, a guidebook used to identify mental disorders.
