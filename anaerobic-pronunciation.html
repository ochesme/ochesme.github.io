20. Differentiate anaerobic alactic and anaerobic lactic system?


An anaerobic alactic system is a system wherein there is an absence of oxygen and no lactic acid is produced. This system only takes 6-15 seconds. With that, ATP (adenosine triphosphate) or the body energy is utilized and made the fastest, that is why energy bursts in a short time. This training mechanism is primarily targeting the increase of maximal strength, speed, and/or power. Example exercises are 100 meters/yard dash, high jumps, and lifting.

An anaerobic lactic system is a system wherein there is an absence of oxygen but lactic acid is produced. This system takes about 2 minutes. With that, ATP or energy is made faster but energy bursts slower in a short time. Example exercises are 800 meters/yard dash and 5 km run.

22. What is aerobes, anaerobes and facultative? Kindly give an example for each types of oxygen the aerobes, anaerobes and facultative.


Answer:

Aerobes are microorganisms that need oxygen to live and grow, like most fungi, some bacteria and certain protozoa.

Anaerobes are microorganisms that can't survive in the presence of oxygen, and use different methods to generate energy, like Clostridium tetani or Bacteroides fragilis.

Facultative organisms can live with or without oxygen and can switch between methods depending on what's available, like Escherichia coli or Salmonella enterica. Knowing what type of oxygen microorganisms need helps in understanding their behavior and how they affect us.
