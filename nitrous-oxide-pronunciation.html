CARBON DIOXIDE:

SOURCE:

- There are both natural and human sources of carbon dioxide emissions. Natural sources include decomposition, ocean release and respiration. Human sources come from activities like cement production, deforestation as well as the burning of fossil fuels like coal, oil and natural gas.

METHANE:

SOURCE:

- Methane is produced by the breakdown or decay of organic material and can be introduced into the atmosphere by either natural processes – such as the decay of plant material in wetlands, the seepage of gas from underground deposits or the digestion of food by cattle – or human activities – such as oil and gas.

USES:

- Methane is an odorless, colorless, flammable gas. It is used primarily as fuel to make heat and light. It is also used to manufacture organic chemicals. Methane can be formed by the decay of natural materials and is common in landfills, marshes, septic systems and sewers.

OXIDE:

SOURCE:

- Nitrogen oxides are produced in combustion processes, partly from nitrogen compounds in the fuel, but mostly by direct combination of atmospheric oxygen and nitrogen in flames. Nitrogen oxides are produced naturally by lightning, and also, to a small extent, by microbial processes in soils.

10. in science, nitrous oxide refers to?​


In science, nitrous oxide refers to?​

The chemical molecule nitrous oxide, sometimes known as laughing gas, nitrous, or nos, is a nitrogen oxide with the formula N2.

2O is a colorless, non-flammable gas having a little metallic odor and taste at ambient temperature. Nitrous oxide, like molecular oxygen, is a potent oxidizer at high temperatures.

Because of its anesthetic and pain-relieving properties, nitrous oxide is widely used in medicine, particularly in surgery and dentistry. Humphry Davy invented the term "laughing gas" to describe the euphoric effects of breathing it, a feature that has led to its recreational usage as a dissociative anesthetic. nitrous oxide is on the World Health Organization's List of Essential Medications, which includes the safest and most effective medicines required in a health system. It is also used as an oxidizer in rocket propellants and in motor racing to boost engine power.

11. A medical cylinder contains a mixture of oxygen and nitrous oxide (N_2 O). The pressure gauge on the tank reads 1.20 atm. If the partial pressure of oxygen is 0.180 atm, what is the partial pressure of nitrous oxide?


Given:

[tex]P_{\text{O}_{2}} = \text{0.180 atm}[/tex]

[tex]P_{\text{T}} = \text{1.20 atm}[/tex]

Unknown:

[tex]P_{\text{N}_{2}\text{O}}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]P_{\text{T}} = P_{\text{O}_{2}} + P_{\text{N}_{2}\text{O}}[/tex]

[tex]P_{\text{N}_{2}\text{O}} = P_{\text{T}} - P_{\text{O}_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]P_{\text{N}_{2}\text{O}} = \text{1.20 atm} - \text{0.180 atm}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{P_{\text{N}_{2}\text{O}} = \text{1.02 atm}}[/tex]

15. Sally switches her car to run on nitrous oxide fuel. The nitrous oxide allows her car to develop 10,000 N of force. What is Sally's acceleration if her car has a mass of 500 kg?​


Formula:

[tex]\boxed{F=ma}[/tex]

where

F = force, m = mass of the object, a = acceleration of the object

Solution:

Solving for the acceleration, we get

[tex]a=\dfrac{F}{m}=\dfrac{10000\text{ N}}{500 \text{ kg}}\\\\\boxed{a=20\text{ m/s}^2}[/tex]

Therefore, the acceleration of Sally's car is 20 m/s².
