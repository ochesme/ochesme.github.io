20. Warren is traveling on her scooter at 2m/s. If she accelerates at a rate of 0.5m/s for 2s, what is her final velocity?​


#BrainlyFast

Problem:

Warren is traveling on her scooter at  2 m/s. If she accelerates at a rate of    0.5 m/s² for 2s, what is her final velocity?

Note:

The formula for acceleration is ( a = Vf - Vi / t ). To solve for final velocity, we have the formula ( Vf = Vi + at ).

Given Data:

Vi ( initial velocity ) = 2 m/s

a ( acceleration ) = 0.5 m/s²

t ( time ) = 2 s

Vf ( final velocity ) = unknown

Solution:

To solve for final velocity, we have the formula ( Vf = Vi + at ).

Vf = Vi + at

    = 2 m/s + (0.5 m/s² × 2 s )

    = 2 m/s + 1 m/s

    = 3 m/s

Answer:

21. After 2.0 s,Isabella was riding her bicycle at 3.0 m/s on a straight path. After 5.0 s,she was moving at 5.4 m/s.What was her acceleration


Answer:

The acceleration can be calculated using the formula:

acceleration = (final velocity - initial velocity) / time elapsed

We can use the information given in the problem to calculate the acceleration:

Initial velocity = 3.0 m/s Final velocity = 5.4 m/s Time elapsed = 5.0 s - 2.0 s = 3.0 s

acceleration = (5.4 m/s - 3.0 m/s) / 3.0 s = 0.9 m/s^2

Therefore, Isabella's acceleration was 0.9 m/s^2.
