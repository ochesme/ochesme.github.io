19. If you lived during the Spanish times, what other customs and traditions would have affected the way you live then?​


Answer:

During the Spanish colonization of the Philippines, many customs and traditions were introduced by the Spanish that affected the way people lived. Here are some examples:

1. Religion: The Spanish introduced Christianity to the Philippines, and it became the dominant religion. Many Filipinos converted to Catholicism and began to practice Catholic customs and traditions.

2. Language: The Spanish language became the official language of the Philippines during the colonial period. Spanish was taught in schools, and many Filipinos learned to speak, read, and write in Spanish.

3. Food and Cuisine: The Spanish introduced many new foods and cooking techniques to the Philippines. Some of these include the use of olive oil, saffron, and paprika, which are still used in Filipino cuisine today.

4. Clothing: The Spanish introduced new styles of clothing to the Philippines, including the Barong Tagalog for men and the Maria Clara dress for women. These styles of clothing have become traditional Filipino attire.

5. Architecture: The Spanish introduced new styles of architecture to the Philippines, including the Baroque style. Many churches and other buildings were built in this style during the colonial period.

Overall, many customs and traditions introduced by the Spanish during the colonial period have had a lasting impact on Filipino culture and continue to influence the way people live today

30. why were animistic rituals and mimetic dances and customs not considered forms of theater by early spanish scholars?I need answer yung maayos 20 points guys​


ANSWER

When the Spaniards reached our shores, they used dramas such as zarzuelas as a pedagogical tool to influence the pagan tribes and teach them about Christianity and religion.

Another important form of theater popularized during the Spanish colonization is the comedia, also known as moro-moro, linambay, or arakyo. It’s a play in verse that portrays the lives, loves, and wars of moors and Christians.

Moro-Moro is a secular comedy that dramatizes the war between Christians and Muslims through the forbidden love between the prince and the princess. The comedy is resolved with the non-Christian being converted to Christianity, or through his or her death, immediately followed by his or her resurrection.

Comedias were normally performed in the pueblos or village centers to attract more people to the foundation of its regime. The comedia can last anywhere from 3 to 15 hours through a series of performances. The first Filipino comedia was performed in Latin and Spanish by Fr. Vicente Puche in Cebu in 1598.

On the other hand, a zarzuela is a form of musical theater that combines spoken word and song that celebrates various Catholic liturgical feasts. Jugar Con Fuego by Francisco Asenjo Barbieri was the first zarzuela introduced in the country in late 1878 or early 1879.

By August 17, 1893, Teatro Zorilla, the home of zarzuela, was inaugurated.

Throughout the 333 years of the Spanish regime, the Philippines was widely influenced by their culture, tradition, and religion. Today, zarzuelas and comedias are still being performed, albeit with other cultural influences and contemporary touches.
