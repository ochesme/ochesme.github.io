4. In a science laboratory, you rub a glass rod with silk, placing a 25-nC positive charge on it. Calculate the force on the rod due to the Earth’s magnetic field (5 10−5 ), if you throw it with a horizontal velocity of 15 m/s west.


The force on the rod : F = 1.875 x 10 ⁻¹¹ N

Further explanation

The magnetic force on a moving charge can be formulated :

F = qvB sin θ

v = the velocity of a charge, m/s

B = the magnetic field, T

F = magnetic force, N

q = charge, C

θ = the angle between the directions of v and B

The magnetic force F is perpendicular to the plane formed by v and B

The direction can be determined by the right-hand rule method

the thumb ⇒ vthe fingers ⇒ Bthe palm ⇒ F

Given

Charge = 25 nC = 25 x 10⁻⁹C

The Earth’s magnetic field =5 x 10⁻⁵ T

Required

The force

Solution

By using the right-hand rule method, we can see that the angle-θ between the velocity(west) and the direction of the field(north) is 90º

The magnetic force

F = 25 x 10⁻⁹C x 15 m/s x 5 x 10⁻⁵ T x sin 90

F = 1.875 x 10 ⁻¹¹ N

[tex]\frac{M}{V} = D[/tex]

[tex]M = VD[/tex]

28. 5. An apparatus is designed to study insects at an acceleration of magnitude 980 m/s². The apparatus consists of a 2.0-m rod with insect containers at either end. The rod rotates about an axis perpendicular to the rod and at its centre. (a) How fast does an insect move when it experiences a radial acceleration of 980 m/s²? (b) What is the angular speed of the insect?​


Answer:

a.)

980 m/s² = v²/1.0 m

v² = 980 m²/s²

v = √980 m/s

v ≈ 31.3 m/s

Therefore, the insect moves at a speed of approximately 31.3 m/s when it experiences a radial acceleration of 980 m/s².

b.)

= 31.3 m/s ÷ 1.0 m

= 31.3 rad/s

Therefore, the angular speed of the insect is approximately 31.3 radians per second.
