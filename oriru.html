9. Learning Task 2 (Week 2)Give the meaning of the following terminologies.1.Washi-2. Origami-3. Noshi-4. Ukiyoe-5. tsutsumi-6. orisue-7. orimono-8. tatamigami-9. Papierfalten-10. Shinto-​


Answer:

1. WASHI- is traditional Japanese paper. The term is used to describe paper that uses local fiber, processed by hand and made in the traditional manner.

2. ORIGAMI- is the art of paper folding, which is often associated with Japanese culture.

3. NOSHI- are a kind of ceremonial origami fold entirely distinct from "origami-tsuki". They are not certificates, but are attached to gifts to express "good wishes".

4. UKIYO-E- is a genre of Japanese art which flourished from the 17th through 19th centuries.

5. TSUTSUMI- is a Japanese businessman . During the Japanese economic bubble, Forbes listed Tsutsumi as the wealthiest person

6. ORISUE- paper folding for play was known by a variety of names, including "orikata", "orisue", "orimono", "tatamigami" and others.

7. ORIMONO- vaginal discharge,menstruation,afterbirth.

8. TATAMIGAMI- a compound of two smaller Japanese words: "ori", meaning to fold, and "kami", meaning paper. ... by a variety of names, including " orikata", "orisue", "orimono", "tatamigami".

9. PAPIER FALTEN- to make paper boats.

10. SHINTO- also known as the kami-no-michi, a religion which originated in Japan. Classified as an East Asian religion by scholars of religion, its practitioners often regard it as Japan's indigenous religion and as a nature religion.
