5. Is there always going to be an equal number of guanine and cytosine


YES, The amount of guanine will be equal to the amount of cytosine.

Detail of explanation:

DNA (Deoxyribonucleic Acid) is the genetic material found on chromosomes in the nucleus (cell nucleus). DNA is a double-helix or chain with two twisted bands facing each other. These two chains are composed of a deoxyribose sugar group, and an amino acid group. There are four types of amino acids, namely: adenine (A), guanine (G), thymine (T) and cytosine (C).

The bonds between these amino acids will connect the two DNA strands. These four amino acids are grouped into purine and pyrimidine bases. Adenine (A) and guanine are the purine bases (G). Thymine (T) and cytosine are the pyrimidine bases (C). In a DNA chain, purine bases must be paired with one pyrimidine base, namely adenine (A) will pair with thymine (T) and guanine (G) will pair with cytosine (S).

Because of this pairing pattern, if we want to calculate the amount of adenine, guanine, thymine, and cytosine in DNA, we know that:

The number of purine bases (adenine plus guanine) is the same as the number of pyrimidine bases (thymine plus cytosine). The total number of amino acids must be even. The amount of adenine will be equal to the amount of thymine, because adenine must only pair with thymine.The amount of guanine will be equal to the amount of cytosine, the same as in the above case, because guanine will only pair with cytosine.
