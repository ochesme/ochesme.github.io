16. what is decentering in philosophy ​


Answer:

Abstract

Decentering is the process by which a child becomes capable of considering more than one aspect of an object or situation at a time. Decentering is considered an important developmental process to obtain the ability to understand things from the other side, or other persons’ perspectives. Through decentering, a child develops a better understanding of reality by incorporating not only his or her own perspectives, but also the perspectives seen from other points of view. In Piaget's theory of intellectual development, decentering occurs in the concrete operations stage and is an essential process for subsequent development of abstract concepts.
