6. An aquarium has the shape of a rectangular prism. Its length is 110 cm. Its width is of its length and its height is of its length. What is the volume of the aquarium?​


Answer:

Let's first determine the width and height of the rectangular prism:

Width = 1/2 x Length = 1/2 x 110 cm = 55 cmHeight = 2/3 x Length = 2/3 x 110 cm = 73.33 cm (rounded to two decimal places)

Now we can use the formula for the volume of a rectangular prism:

Volume = Length x Width x HeightVolume = 110 cm x 55 cm x 73.33 cmVolume = 438,716.5 cubic centimeters (rounded to one decimal place)

Therefore, the volume of the aquarium is approximately 438,716.5 cubic centimeters.

24. What do you expect to happen to the volume of a gas if its pressure is doubled and its temperature is reduced to half?a. its volume is increasedb. its volume is doubledc. its volume remains unchangedd. its volume is decreased


The correct answer is letter d. its volume is decreased. It is notable that the gas volume is inversely proportional to its pressure and directly proportional to its temperature. With this key points, we can understand that:

If the pressure of a gas had doubled (increased), the volume of a gas will decrease.If the temperature is reduced to half (colder), the volume will also reduce.

Thus, the total volume of the gas will decrease.

The Pressure and Volume Relationship

It is known that as the gas pressure increases, the gas volume decreases. It is because the particles of gas are forced to be closer together. We used the word conversely, as the gas pressure decreases, the volume of a gas increases. It is because the particles of gas can now move apart more farther.

The Temperature and Volume Relationship

It is known that gases normally expanding when they are heated. It is because the same amount of substance will now occupy a greater volume. The volume of a gas will likely to increase.

You need to know that:

Conversely proportional means the other way around. If one variable will decrease, the other variable will increase and vice versa.Directly proportional means the same way around. If one variable will decrease, the other variable will also decrease and vice versa.

27. When an object is released from rest. and falls in the absence of friction; which of the following is true concerning its motion? Both its acceleration and its velocity are constant: Neither its acceleration nor its velocity is constant: Its velocity is constant: Its acceleration is constant:


When an object is released from rest and falls in the absence of friction. Its acceleration is constant:

Free-fall motion

Free fall motion is a downward vertical motion with no initial velocity, only influenced by the earth's gravity. All objects in free fall have the same acceleration, which is equal to the acceleration due to gravity on the earth which is given the symbol g.

Called free fall because this motion is free from any thrust so that the initial velocity is zero. This is different from the motion of objects on the floor for example, where objects must be pushed in order to start moving. In free fall motion, the object is not pushed to fall down, but the object begins to move down by itself, without being pushed. So, the object is just released, and then the object will fall down.

If two balls of the same size but masses of 250 grams and 750 grams are dropped from the roof of a building, which will hit the ground first.Perhaps you will answer that the object with the greater mass will reach the ground faster. Thus, the ball with a mass of 750 grams will reach the ground first. Apparently, your answer is the same as the statement of a famous philosopher, Aristotle.

Aristotle stated that in downward motion, an object that has a greater weight will accelerate in proportion to its weight. This statement seems quite reasonable, where if a piece of paper and a pebble are dropped at the same time, it is found that the pebbles reach the ground faster. than paper. Therefore, Aristotle's opinion lasted quite a long time, up to several centuries.

It was not until the time of Galileo Galilei (1564–1642), that Aristotle's opinion was questioned. Galileo stated that if gravity only acts on an object, each object falling down will experience the same acceleration, so that if two different objects are dropped simultaneously, both will reach the ground at the same time.

An object in free fall is not affected by the mass and size of the object. As an illustration, a marble with a throw ball when dropped from the same height simultaneously will hit the ground with the same speed simultaneously.
