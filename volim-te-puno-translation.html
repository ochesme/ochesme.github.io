13. In a commercial airliner, an empty water bottle contains about 500 ml of air. The air pressure at the altitude is 0.74 atm. What will the volime of the water bottle be after landing and reaching an air pressure of 1.0 atm?


Good Day...

Given data:

V1 (initial volume) = 500 ml                              V2 (final volume) = ? unknown

P1 (initial pressure) = .74 atm                            P2 (final pressure) = 1.0 atm

Solution:

This problem can be solved using Boyle's Law which states that volume is inversely proportional to pressure when temperature and the amount of gas are constant.

Formula of Boyle's Law is P1V1 = P2V2

To solve for V2 we can used V2 = P1V1 / P2

V2 = P1V1 / P2

    = .74 atm × 500 ml / 1.0 atm    (cancel the unit atm)

    = 370 ml

Answer:

The volume after landing is 370 ml
