14. state the 4 conditions of a deadlock in operating system​


Answer:

Explanation:

1. Mutual Exclusion-

 

By this condition,

There must exist at least one resource in the system which can be used by only one process at a time.

If there exists no such resource, then deadlock will never occur.

Printer is an example of a resource that can be used by only one process at a time.

 

2. Hold and Wait-

 

By this condition,

There must exist a process which holds some resource and waits for another resource held by some other process.

 

3. No Preemption-

 

By this condition,

Once the resource has been allocated to the process, it can not be preempted.

It means resource can not be snatched forcefully from one process and given to the other process.

The process must release the resource voluntarily by itself.

 

4. Circular Wait-

 

By this condition,

All the processes must wait for the resource in a cyclic manner where the last process waits for the resource held by the first process.

He has only P50 million cash in the bank. He invited two close friends to chip in the balance of P50 million to make up for the balance. Mr. Han wants to be elected as president and his wife as treasurer of the company. His two friends would be elected vice-president and corporate secretary, respectively. While Mr. Han, his wife and son, the manager of the planned branch of the grocery store, would control the day to day operations of the company, he does not feel comfortable with the 50-50 sharing of the capital. What if his relations with his two friends turn sour in the future? Aggressive and ambitious that Mr. Han is, what worries him is if he further expands the grocery business in the future ad the two friends do not agree, there would be a deadlock in the voting. His two friends could be a bar to his future expansion plans. Mr. Han decided to go on a 51-49 percent sharing in the stock holdings of the planned company. 51 percent is to be owned and controlled by him. He needs therefore P2 million more for his paid up capital to assure control of the company. His problem is, where would he get that much money fast? Which sources of funds could Mr. Han avail of on credit? Explain your answer.


Answer:

Mr. Han needs P2 million more to meet the requirement of a subscribed and paid-up capital of P100 million for his planned company. He wants to maintain control of 51% of the stock holdings of the company, but he only has P50 million in cash, which is insufficient to meet the capitalization requirements.

Mr. Han could avail of the following sources of funds on credit:

1.Bank loans - Mr. Han could apply for a loan from a commercial bank. Banks usually require collateral for loans, such as real estate or other assets, to secure the loan. If Mr. Han has valuable assets, he could use them to secure the loan.

2.Supplier credit - Mr. Han could negotiate with his suppliers to extend payment terms, allowing him to delay payments until after he generates revenue from sales. This would increase his working capital and provide funds for his company's capitalization.

3.Trade credit - Mr. Han could negotiate with his customers to pay him on extended credit terms, allowing him to generate more cash inflow and increase his working capital. However, this would require Mr. Han to have reliable and creditworthy customers who would pay on time.

4.Factoring - Mr. Han could sell his receivables to a factoring company, which would provide him with immediate cash for a fee. This would allow him to generate funds without increasing his debt.

5.Crowdfunding - Mr. Han could consider crowdfunding platforms, where he could present his business plan and request funding from interested investors in exchange for equity in his company. However, this would require a lot of effort in marketing and promoting his business to attract investors.

6.Overall, Mr. Han has several options to secure additional funds for his planned company's capitalization. He needs to evaluate each option's pros and cons, including the interest rates, repayment terms, and collateral requirements, to make an informed decision on the best financing solution for his needs.
