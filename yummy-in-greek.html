13. does saliva make food yummy


Saliva, which is important in taste sensation, is composed of 99% water and 1 % enzyme important for digestion. Simple sugars dissolve readily in water. They require little digestion and are quickly absorbed into the bloodstream. Starches are also relatively simple and are converted to sugars in the mouth. While sugars and starches quickly can be prepared to go into the bloodstream, most foods are more complex requiring further help from our saliva, before we can identify them as tasty. The digestive system produces many different chemicals called enzymes that help digest nutrients. For example, the amylase aids the water molecules in our saliva to split the chemical bonds between the monosaccharides in starch. Simple sugar units are released then bind to “sweet” receptors, which communicate the message to the brain that the food is certainly nutritious and is safe to swallow.
