29. Directions: (by group: max 4 members) Below are the different types of water treatment method. Using a presentation slides, define each of the terms in each slide. Provide a process flow or image of the technology for each treatment method. •Screening •Plain sedimentation Sedimentation aided with coagulation •Filtration •Disinfection •Aeration •Softening •Defluoridation •Recarbonation desalination etc. •Sludge Drying pH CorrectionPAHELP MGA PAR...NEED SANA NG REFERENCES ​


Answer:

1. Screening: This is the first step in the water treatment process, and involves the removal of large debris and particles from the water. This is usually done using screens or mesh filters.

2. Plain sedimentation: This process involves allowing water to sit undisturbed in a large tank, allowing heavier particles and sediment to settle to the bottom. The clearer water is then removed from the top of the tank.

3. Sedimentation aided with coagulation: This process involves the addition of a coagulant, such as alum or ferric chloride, to the water to help bind smaller particles together into larger clumps, which then settle more easily in the sedimentation tank.

4. Filtration: This process involves passing the water through a series of filters, typically made of sand, gravel, and activated carbon, to remove smaller particles, impurities, and organic matter.

5. Disinfection: This process involves the use of chemicals or other methods to kill or remove harmful microorganisms, such as bacteria, viruses, and parasites, from the water. Common disinfection methods include chlorination, ozonation, and ultraviolet (UV) radiation.

6. Aeration: This process involves exposing the water to air, which helps to remove dissolved gases, such as hydrogen sulfide and carbon dioxide, and can also help to oxidize certain minerals and organic compounds.

7. Softening: This process involves the removal of minerals, such as calcium and magnesium, which can cause water hardness and scale buildup in pipes and appliances. Softening can be done using ion exchange resins, which exchange calcium and magnesium ions for sodium ions.

8. Defluoridation: This process involves the removal of excess fluoride from drinking water, which can be harmful in high concentrations. Defluoridation can be done using adsorption, precipitation, or ion exchange methods.

9. Recarbonation: This process involves the addition of carbon dioxide to the water to adjust the pH and improve taste and clarity.

10. Desalination: This process involves the removal of salt and other minerals from seawater or brackish water, making it safe for drinking and other uses. Desalination can be done using either thermal distillation or membrane filtration methods.

11. Sludge drying: This process involves the removal of excess water from sludge, which is the solid waste material produced during the water treatment process. Sludge drying can be done using a variety of methods, including centrifugation, evaporation, and drying beds.
