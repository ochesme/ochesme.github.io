2.Filtration is the process of separating suspended solid matter from a liquid, by causing the latter to pass through the pores of some substance, called a filter. The liquid which has passed through the filter is called the filtrate.

15. What is ex-situ and In-situ conservation.


In-situ and ex-situ conservation are both conservation practices where the goal is to protect a particular species from going extinct and promote the flourishing of the said species. In-situ conservation means that the particular organism, a plant or an animal will be protected in the wild, where they live. Meanwhile, ex-situ conservation means that they will be taken out of the wild and transferred to a zoo or other similar facilities. Ex-situ conservation is usually done if the concerned organism could not survive on their own in the wild, mainly due to harsh conditions or the presence of too much predators.  

The process could also be combined, when for example, an animal is initially grown and bred in a zoo and then released eventually into the wild. This is the usual case of the Philippines Eagle, where they are taken care of prior to being released. Sadly, some of these birds end up being dead.

19. what it means SINUS Tacety CADIA​


Answer:

Sinus Tachycardja

What is sinus tachycardia?

Sinus tachycardia refers to a faster-than-usual heart rhythm. Your heart has a natural pacemaker called the sinus node, which generates electrical impulses that move through your heart muscle and cause it to contract, or beat.

When these electrical impulses are transmitted normally, it’s referred to as normal sinus rhythm. Normal sinus rhythm typically results in a heart rate of 60 to 100 beats per minute.

Sometimes, these electrical impulses are sent out faster than normal, causing sinus tachycardia, which often results in a heart rate of over 100 beats per minute.
