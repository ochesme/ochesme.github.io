10. What is the energy conversion of TV Energy Conversion of tv


Answer:

Energy conversion also termed energy transformation, is the process of changing one form of energy into another.

A television set is another device that operates by the transformation of energy.

An electrical beam from the back of the television tube strikes a thin layer of chemicals on the television screen, causing them to glow.

In this case, electrical energy is converted into light.

Hence, the energy transformation in television is from electrical energy to light energy.

Explanation:

One form of energy can be transformed into another form. This phenomenon is called energy transformation. When we turn on the television, electrical energy changes into light and sound.

26. The diagonal of a TV is 15 inches. The width of a TV is 10 inches. What is the height of the TV?​


Answer:

Hence, The height of the TV is 11.18 inches

Step-by-step explanation:

The diagonal is the hypotenuse of a right triangle. So, The diagonal of the TV, width, and height we would make the right angle triangle.

Then, we apply pythagoras theorem.

Given : [tex]\begin{gathered}\boxed{\begin{array}{l}d(\bold{diagonal}) = 15 \\ w(\bold{width}) = 10 \\ h(\bold{height}) = \: \: ? \end{array}}\end{gathered}[/tex]

Using Pythagoras Theorem,

[tex]d^{2} = w^{2} + h^{2}[/tex]

[tex]h = \sqrt{(15)^{2} - (10)^{2}}[/tex]

[tex]h = \sqrt{225 - 100}[/tex]

[tex]h = \sqrt{125}[/tex]

[tex]h = 11.18 \: inches[/tex]

Therefore, The required height is total [tex]11.18[/tex] inches
