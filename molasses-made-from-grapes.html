6. From 1960 to 1980, the consumer price index (CPI) increased from 29.6 to 82.4 If a pound of grapes cost $0.14 in 1960 and the price of grapes increased at the same rate as the CPI from 1960 to 1980, approximately how much did the price of a pound of grapes cost increase from 1960 to 1980?


Since the pound of grapes increased at the same rate as the consumer price index (CPI) from 1960 to 1980, we must first find the rate of increase.

Now it says that the CPI increased from 29.6 to 82.4. To get the rate of increase, we simply have to divide 82.4/29.6 = 2.783783...

Now we just multiply this same rate to the $0.14 price of grapes in 1960.

$0.14 x 2.783783... = $0.3897..., which is approximately $0.39.

But since the question asks for the increase, we subtract the old price from the new price: $0.39 - $0.14 = $0.25.

9. how to make molasses


Explanation:

The most popular way to make molasses is to use sugarcane. Strip each cane of its leaves with a knife, then crush the cane to produce a thick, light green juice. Next, strain the juice through a cheesecloth and pour it into a boiler pan. Bring the liquid to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer it for 6 hours.

13. what is the intermolecular force of molasses


Answer:

Molasses consist of water and carbohydrates (sugar). Due to the presence of OH group in sugar molecule intense hydrogen bonding is found in them. Therefore, stronger intermolecular forces are present in molasses than water. These strong intermolecular forces in the molasses cause resistance in the movement. Hence, slower movement than water is found in molasses
