1. look for definition of the following words wrire your vocabulary in yor notetaking notebook 1 cell 2tissues 3organs 4organ system 5organism 6population 7community 8ecosystem 9biome 10biosphere​


Answer:

1. the smallest structural and functional unit of an organism, typically microscopic and consisting of cytoplasm and a nucleus enclosed in a membrane. Microscopic organisms typically consist of a single cell, which is either eukaryotic or prokaryotic.

2. A group or layer of cells that work together to perform a specific function.

3. a part of an organism that is typically self-contained and has a specific vital function, such as the heart or liver in humans.

"the internal organs"

4. An organ is a group of tissues that work together for the overall function of the organ, and an organ system is a group of organs that work together to perform a specific function. The human organism consists of eleven organ systems.

5.an individual living thing that carries on the activities of life by means of organs which have separate functions but are dependent on each other : a living person, plant, or animal. organismic.

6. A population is a subset of individuals of one species that occupies a particular geographic area and, in sexually reproducing species, interbreeds. The geographic boundaries of a population are easy to establish for some species but more difficult for others.

7. Community, in biology, is defined as an interacting group of various species in a shared/ common location. A community is a diverse group of organisms that interact in a common location.

8. An ecosystem consists of all the organisms and the physical environment with which they interact. These biotic and abiotic components are linked together through nutrient cycles and energy flows. Energy enters the system through photosynthesis and is incorporated into plant tissue.

9. A biome is a biogeographical unit consisting of a biological community that has formed in response to the physical environment in which they are found and a shared regional climate. Biomes may span more than one continent. Biome is a broader term than habitat and can comprise a variety of habitats.

10. Encyclopedic Entry Vocabulary. The biosphere is made up of the parts of Earth where life exists. The biosphere extends from the deepest root systems of trees, to the dark environment of ocean trenches, to lush rainforests and high mountaintops. Scientists describe Earth in terms of spheres.
