22. 1. Boys undergo maturing of genital organs earlier than girls.​


Answer:

No it isn't, girls undergo maturing of genital organs earlier than boys.

Answer:

This statement is generally true. The onset of puberty in boys typically occurs between the ages of 9 and 14, with the average age being around 11.5 years. During puberty, boys experience a growth spurt, the development of facial and pubic hair, an increase in muscle mass, and a deepening of the voice. They also undergo maturation of the genital organs, which includes the growth of the testes, scrotum, and penis, and the production of sperm.

In contrast, girls typically begin puberty between the ages of 8 and 13, with the average age being around 10.5 years. During puberty, girls experience breast development, the growth of pubic and underarm hair, and the onset of menstruation. They also undergo maturation of the genital organs, which includes the growth and development of the ovaries, uterus, and vagina.

So, while both boys and girls undergo maturation of their genital organs during puberty, boys typically begin this process earlier than girls.
