6. Rum is essentially just an aqueous solution of ethanol. Most of the rum sold commercially is bottled at 80 proof, which means that it is 40% ethanol by volume. What is the molarity of ethanol in rum? The molecular weight of ethanol is 46.07 g/mol, and its density is 0.789 g/mL.


Answer:

The molarity of ethanol in rum is 6.84 M.

Explanation:

To find the molarity of ethanol in rum, we need to first determine the concentration of ethanol in the solution.

We know that the rum is 40% ethanol by volume, which means that in 100 mL of rum, there are 40 mL of ethanol. Using the density of ethanol, we can convert this to grams:

40 mL ethanol x 0.789 g/mL = 31.56 g ethanol

Now we can calculate the number of moles of ethanol present in this amount:

31.56 g ethanol / 46.07 g/mol = 0.684 mol ethanol

Finally, we need to calculate the molarity of ethanol in the solution. Since we know that the volume of the solution is 100 mL, or 0.1 L, we can use the following formula:

Molarity = moles of solute / liters of solution

Molarity = 0.684 mol / 0.1 L = 6.84 M
