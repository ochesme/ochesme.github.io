1. kanino dapat mapunta ang senkaku island?


Answer:

Ang Senkaku Islands ay grupo ng maliliit na isla sa East China Sea.

Ang mga isla ay dapat mapunta sa Japan, dahil ang isla ay pinakamalapit sa Ishigaki, Hatoma, Yonaguni at Iriomote islands ng bansang Japan at sa pagbili ng Japanese Government dito.

Ngunit pinaglalabanan din ng Taiwan at China ang pagmamay-ari dito simula pa noong 15th century.


2. definition of dengaku​


Answer:

Nasu Dengaku is a classic Japanese side dish made with eggplant sliced in half, scored and brushed with a sweet and savory miso glaze. The translation literally means eggplant grilled over a fire, which is exactly how it's done in Japan.

11. A japanese term referring to nature. Select one: a. Kigo b. Keikaku c. Kisho d. Keigo?


Keigo is a Japanese term that can be associate to nature.  To be precise Keigo is a word or phrase associated with a particular season in Japanese poetry. They give an economy of expression that is especially relevant in the very short haiku, as well as the longer linked-verse forms renku and renga, to indicate the season referenced in the poem or stanza. The connection of kigo with a particular season may be obvious, though sometimes it is more subtle. It can be observed that season has long been important in Japanese culture and poetry.

Japanese Season

The traditional Japanese seasons are:

Spring that starts on 4 February—5 May.Summer that starts on 6 May—7 August.Autumn that starts on 8 August—6 November.Winter that starts on  7 November—3 February.

Categorizing Kigo

For Kigo, each season is divided into the early, middle, and late periods, as follows:

Early spring starts on 4 February–5 MarchMid-spring starts on 6 March–4 AprilLate spring starts on 5 April–5 MayEarly summer starts on 6 May–5 JuneMid-summer starts on 6 June–6 JulyLate summer starts on 7 July–7 AugustEarly autumn starts on 8 August–7 SeptemberMid-autumn starts on8 September–7 OctoberLate autumn starts on 8 October–6 NovemberEarly winter starts on 7 November–6 DecemberMid-winter starts on 7 December–4 JanuaryLate winter starts on 5 January–3 February

12. A japanese term referring to nature. select one: a. kisho b. keikaku c. kigo d. keigo


Answer:

None of the above, because the correct answer for a Japanese term referring to nature is “shizen.”  

Explanation:

a. kisho – it means the one who knows his own mind

b. keikaku – it means plan or project or scheme

c. kigo – it indicates a particular season

d. keigo – it means respectful Japanese

However, if you really need to choose among the four choices, the closest answer could be “kigo” as it indicates a particular season.

But bear in mind that the correct answer is really “shizen.”

Shizen is a Japanese word which means nature, natural, or spontaneous. We thought “nature” only pertains to our surrounding that are not man-made. However, Japanese definition of “nature” goes beyond that as it also includes human intervention. We are part of nature. The concept is that we can become one with nature. We go with the flow, meaning there is spontaneity or naturalness.
