9. If 3 books are picked at random from a shelf containing 5 novels, 3 books of poems, and a dictionary, what is the probability that (a) the dictionary is selected


Answer:

1/3 or 0.333

Explanation:

We need to find the probability that a dictionary is selected. Among books on the shelf, there is 1 dictionary. Because the order is not important. We need to use combinations to calculate the number of ways that we can choose 1 out of 1 dictionary.

[tex]\left(\begin{array}{ccc}1\\1\\\end{array}\right) = \frac{1!}{(1-1)!} = 1[/tex]

Among 9 books on the shelf, there is 1 dictionary, so there are 8 non-dictionaries. The number of ways to choose 2 out of 8 non-dictionaries is

[tex]\left(\begin{array}{ccc}2\\8\\\end{array}\right) = \frac{8!}{2! (8-2)!} = 28[/tex]

If the first operation can be performed in 1 way (the number of ways to choose 1 out 1 dictionary) and for each of these ways the second operation can be performed in 28 ways (the number of ways to choose 2 out of 8 non-dictionaries), then these two operations can be performed together in 1 .28 = 28 ways.

So, 1 dictionary and 2 non-dictionaries can be selected in 28 ways.

*The probability of choosing 1 dictionary among 3 selected books is

[tex]P= \frac{The \ number \ of \ ways \ to \ choose \ 3 \ books \ among \ which \ there \ will \ be \ 1 \ dictionary \ and \ 2 \ non-dictionaries}{The\ number \ of \ ways \ to \ choose \ 3 \ out \ of \ 9 \ books}[/tex]

Hence, the required probability is

[tex]P = \frac{28}{4} = \frac{1}{3} = 0.333[/tex]

18. similarities dictionary and English book​


Dictionary

a reference work on a particular subject, the items of which are typically arranged in alphabetical order.

(2)

a book or electronic resource that lists the words of a language (typically in alphabetical order) and gives their meaning, or gives the equivalent words in a different language, often also providing information about pronunciation, origin, and usage.

English Book

a set of written sheets of skin or paper or tablets of wood or ivory.

Similarities:

They are similar in they are both “reference” books. They are different, however, in what it is they reference and how they present material.
