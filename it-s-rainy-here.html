12. The weather forecast says that the probability of it being cloudy tomorrow is 30%, the probability of being rainy is 15%, and the probability of it being both cloudy and rainy is 14%. What is the probability of it being rainy, if you know it will be cloudy?​


PROBABILITY

The weather forecast says that the probability of it being cloudy tomorrow is 30%, the probability of being rainy is 15%, and the probability of it being both cloudy and rainy is 14%. What is the probability of it being rainy, if you know it will be cloudy?

Solution :-Let event A = being cloudy.Let even B = being rainy.

Given probabilities :

Probability of being cloudy = 30%.Probability of being rainy = 15%.Probability of being cloudy and rainy = 14%.

Therefore:

[tex] \qquad \sf \implies{P(A) = 0.3}[/tex]

[tex] \qquad \sf \implies{P(B) = 0.15}[/tex]

[tex] \qquad \sf \implies{P(A \cap B) = 0.14} \\ [/tex]

Conditional Probability Formula :

[tex] \qquad \qquad \sf \implies{\sf P(B|A)=\dfrac{P(A \cap B)}{P(A)}} \\ [/tex]

The probability of being rainy given it is cloudy = P(B | A).

Substitute the given values into the formula :

[tex] \sf\implies \sf P(B|A)=\dfrac{0.14}{0.3}=0.46666...=46.67\%\;(2\;d.p.) \\ [/tex]

[tex] \therefore[/tex] The probability of it being rainy if you know it will be cloudy is about 46.67%.
