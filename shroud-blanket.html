Answer:

10 feet

Step-by-step explanation:

l = 2w + 4, A = 30, w = w

w × l = A

w(2w + 4) = 30

2w² + 4w = 30

2w² + 4w - 30 = 0

(2w - 6)(w + 5) = 0

2w = 6

[tex] \frac{2w}{2} = \frac{6}{2} [/tex]

w = 3

Find l:

w = 3, l = 2w + 4

2(3) + 4 = 10

l = 10

23. helen finished knitting a blanket. she wanted the length of the blanket to be 9cm longer than twice the width of the blanket. if the area of the blanket is 200cm^2, find the dimensions of the blanket? ​


Answer:

L = 25

W = 8

Solution:[tex]L = 9 +2W[/tex][tex](9+2W)(W)=200[/tex][tex]9W+2W^2=200[/tex]

Subtract 200 to both sides

[tex]9W + 2W^2 - 200 = 0[/tex]

Change the places of [tex]2W^2[/tex] and [tex]9W[/tex] to form it like [tex]ax^2 + bx + c = 0[/tex] that can be solved using quadratic formula: [tex]x = \frac{-b±\sqrt{b^2-4ac} }{2a}[/tex]

[tex]2W^2 + 9W - 200 = 0[/tex]

Thus, [tex]w = \frac{-9±\sqrt{9^2-4(2)(-200)} }{2(2)}[/tex]

[tex]w =\frac{-9±\sqrt{1681} }{2(2)}[/tex]

[tex]w =\frac{-9±41 }{4}[/tex]

We will ignore the negative answer because there are no dimensions of any figure that is negative.

Thus, [tex]w = \frac{32}{4} = 8[/tex]

Since we already found the width, we can now find the length.

[tex]L = 9 + 2W[/tex]

[tex]L = 9 + 2(8) = 25[/tex]

To check if the answer is correct, lets multiply it

25 × 8 = 200, which is the area of the blanket.
