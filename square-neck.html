9. A square-shaped handkerchief measures 16 inches on each side. you fold it along its diagonal so you can tie it around your neck. how long is this tie?


Answer:

The tie is 22.63 inches long.

Step-by-step explanation:

Diagonal of a Square

The diagonal of a square is the line stretching from one corner of the square to the opposite corner.

How to find the length of the diagonal of a square?

To find the length of the diagonal of a square, multiply the length of one side by the square root of 2.

Going back to the problem above, the handkerchief measures 16 inches on each side.

To find the length of its diagonal, we need to multiply 16 by the square root of 2.

Equation:

16√2

Solution:

(Using a calculator is the best way to find the answer.)

16√2

= 22.627416997969

Round off to two decimal places.

Final Answer:

22.63 inches

C Giraffes with longer necks survived because they were better suited to the environment. D Long-necked giraffes mated only with other long-necked giraffes. 


How is natural selection in the evolution of long necks in giraffes best explained?

The natural selection in the evolution of long necks in giraffes is best explained by the answer which is Letter(C) Giraffes with longer necks survived because they were better suited to the environment.

There about nine species and subspecies of giraffes spread throughout Africa that have long necks because they all evolved in environments (dry savannas) that require them to reach high up in the trees for food to survive. A popular food is the Acacia leaves which they pick off with their prehensile tongue. The forest giraffe called the Okapi does not have this feature since it is able to survive deep in the jungle by eating low lying leaves, fruits, algae, and grasses.

21. what is the meaning of strap neck or upper neck​


The infrahyoid muscles, or strap muscles, are a group of four pairs of muscles in the anterior (frontal) part of the neck. The four infrahyoid muscles aresternohyoid, sternothyroid, thyrohyoid and omohyoid muscles. Excluding the sternothyroid, the infrahyoid muscles either originate from or insert on to the hyoid bone.
