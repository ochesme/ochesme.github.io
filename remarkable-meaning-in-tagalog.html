Ang pangwakas na talumpati ay isang mahalagang elemento para sa isang tao kapag gumagawa ng isang talumpati. Ang talumpating madaling tandaan ay isang talumpating may halaga at bigat na maaaring tanggapin ng maraming tao. Gayunpaman, kahit na ang talumpating binigkas ay mabigat ngunit hindi isinara ng isang kawili-wiling pangwakas na talumpati, kung gayon ito ay kadalasang madaling makalimutan.

Karaniwang nakikipagkumpitensya ang mga tao sa paggamit ng mga kawili-wiling pangwakas na talumpati. Gayunpaman, ito ay karaniwang hindi maaaring gawin nang kusang-loob. Samakatuwid, ang mga pangwakas na salita ng kawili-wiling talumpating ito ay napakahalagang malaman. Kung maaari kang magbigay ng talumpati ngunit hindi naiintindihan ang mga kawili-wiling pangwakas na talumpati, kailangan mong malaman ang impormasyon tungkol sa mga kawili-wiling pangwakas na talumpati.

Sa Pasko, ang mga Kristiyano ay karaniwang nagdaraos ng iba't ibang aktibidad upang ipagdiwang ang masayang sandali na ito. Karaniwan bago simulan ang isang aktibidad, isang tao ang hinirang upang magbigay ng talumpati. Ang mga pahayag ay ipinarating upang malaman ng mga manonood ang layunin at layunin ng gaganaping kaganapan. Gayunpaman, hindi kakaunti ang nahihirapang gumawa ng serye ng mga pagbati sa Pasko. 10. _____2. it means the art of singing and dancing characterized by remarkable acting, music, dance and brilliantly colored settings and was found by Izumo no Okuni​


Answer:

Explanation:

Kabuki, traditional Japanese popular drama with singing and dancing performed in a highly stylized manner. A rich blend of music, dance, mime, and spectacular staging and costuming, it has been a major theatrical form in Japan for four centuries. The term kabuki originally suggested the unorthodox and shocking character of this art form. In modern Japanese, the word is written with three characters: ka, signifying “song”; bu, “dance”; and ki, “skill.”

Okuni, also called Izumo no Okuni, (flourished 17th century), Japanese dancer who is credited as being the founder of the Kabuki art form. Although many extant contemporary sources such as paintings, drawings, and diaries have shed light on Okuni’s life, the accuracy of such primary sources has been difficult to establish. Welcome words are speeches spoken at celebrations, parties, and others. The meaning of the opening speech is a sentence that is delivered before the main event begins. Generally it contains a welcome from the presenter and is continued by important parties or figures who give advice or hope to the audience.

Types of GreetingsThe opening remarks are usually spoken by the master of ceremonies before inviting stakeholders to give official remarks.Briefing remarks, namely remarks that direct the audience to follow the event as well as possible. Usually read by the host.The inauguration speech, can be in the form of a spontaneous speech without text or using a speech text that has been prepared on a piece of paper.Accountability remarks, namely the opening speech that opens an accountability report on events or activities that have been or have been held.
