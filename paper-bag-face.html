Cotton

Cotton is a light solid object which can absorb water. Before adding water, cotton is shaped like a collection of fine fibers. Some Gossypium species use a fine fabric called cotton to coat their seeds. Cotton fiber is made of plant fibers, which are also known as cellulose fibers in nature and are extracted from fruit. A pure and natural cellulose polymer is present in cotton fiber. Because of the way this cellulose is organized, cotton has a special blend of strength, toughness, and absorbency.

Tissue Paper

Tissue paper is lightweight or crepe paper with a low weight with thin sheet form and can absorb water very well. Tissue paper Real paper pulp or recycled paper pulp can both be used to make tissue paper. The fibers extracted from the pulp are compressed to create the thin material known as paper. The fibers used often contain cellulose and hemicellulose and are natural. Paper tissue, also known as tissue paper, is a substance similar to tissue with a soft tint in general, but it resembles a sheet of cloth (made of 100% polyester), making it slick, velvety, and resistant to tearing.

Sponge

Sponges are lightweight solids with extra-pores form with squishy form. Sponges are excellent at absorbing water and water-based solutions. Sponges are cleaning aids made of soft and porous materials. Usually used to clean watertight surfaces.

Face Towel

A face towel in the form of a thick sheet of cloth made of spun yarn that is not tightly so that the towel material can absorb water well.

Sando Bag

Sando bag shaped like a thin sheet and can't absorb water. Sando bag is a bag made of plastic or polyolefin (polyvinyl chloride).
