28. Contains a u- or an s-shaped nucleus; granules stain very dark; releases histamine and heparin.


Answer: mast cell

Explanation: Mast cells contain a u- or s-shaped nucleus and are characterized by the presence of granules that stain very dark. They are known for their role in immune defense, as they release histamine and heparin in response to various stimuli, such as the presence of allergens or pathogens. These substances help to initiate an inflammatory response, which helps to protect the body from infection and injury. Mast cells are also involved in other processes, such as tissue repair and blood clotting.
