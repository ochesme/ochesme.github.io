11. 2. Negros Island the island was first called Buglas a name of a grass comparable to sugarcane.​


Answer:

Ethnic Origin of the People

When the Spanish explorers landed in Negros Oriental in 1565, they found natives who called the place “Buglas”, named after a kind of tall grass resembling the present-day sugar cane plant. Buglas grass was then abundantly growing in the island.
