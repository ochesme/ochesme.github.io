4. are tarsiers mammals??


Answer:

Tarsiers are haplorrhine primates of the family Tarsiidae, which is itself the lone extant family within the infraorder Tarsiiformes. 1. The Philippine tarsier is capable of jumping to a height of 2.0 m in hunting for food. Determine the take off speed of the tarsier.

We have the following data:

Vf (final velocity) = 0 m/s (at maximum height)

Vi (initial velocity) = unknown

g (acceleration of gravity) ≈ -9.8 m / s²

h (height) = 2.0 m

Solution:

To solve this problem, we can use the formula Vf² = Vi²  + 2ad, to solve for Vi,  we can use the formula Vi = √Vf² - 2ad

Vi = √Vf² - 2ad

     = √0 - (2× -9.8 m / s² × 2.0 m)

    = √0 - (-39.2 m²/s²)

    = √39.2 m²/s²

    = 6.26 m/s

Answer:

The take off speed is 6.26 m/s

16. The philippine tarsier can jump to a height of 1.5m in hunting for food. Determine the takeoff speed of the tarsier


Using the formula for velocity

[tex] v = \sqrt{2gh} [/tex]

Where:

v = velocity

g = gravitational constant

[tex]g= 9.8 \: {m} {/}{s}^{2} [/tex]

h = height

Given:

[tex]g = 9.8 \: m {/} {s}^{2} [/tex]

[tex]h = 1.5 \: m[/tex]

Required

[tex]v = {?}[/tex]

Solution

[tex]v = \sqrt{2gh} \\ v = \sqrt{(2)(9.8)(1.5)} \\ v = \sqrt{29.4} \\ v = 5.4221766847 \: m{/}s\\ v = 5 \: m{/}s[/tex]
