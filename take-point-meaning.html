Answer:

WHAT IS FACT?

A fact is a piece of information that is true and real. It is something that can be proven or verified through evidence or observation. Facts are objective and do not depend on personal opinions or beliefs. They are the foundation of knowledge and understanding, and they help us make informed decisions. Facts can be found in various sources, such as books, articles, research studies, and even personal experiences. It is important to distinguish between facts and opinions, as opinions are subjective and can vary from person to person. In order to have a clear and accurate understanding of the world around us, it is crucial to rely on facts.

WHAT IS OPINION?

Opinion is a subjective view or belief that an individual holds about a particular topic, issue, or situation. It is not necessarily based on facts or evidence, and may not be true or real. Opinions are often expressed through words, whether spoken or written, and can vary greatly depending on a person's experiences, values, and beliefs. While opinions can be influenced by external factors such as media or peer pressure, they ultimately stem from an individual's own thoughts and feelings. It is important to recognize that opinions are not necessarily facts, and that different people may hold different opinions on the same subject.
