Ethics is a discipline of philosophy concerned with human behavior, specifically the behavior of people in society. It is derived from the Greek word "ethos," which means "way of life". Ethics investigates the intellectual basis for our moral judgments; it investigates what is morally right or wrong, just or unjust.

Ethics is a set of rules that helps us distinguish between what is right and what is wrong, what is good and what is harmful. Ethics may provide us with meaning’ful and practical advice in our daily lives. Ethical principles (such as honesty, integrity, and accountability) enable us to navigate a road that allows us to cope with ethical quandaries more effectively by removing actions that do not fit our sense of right and wrong - our best rational interests - without compromising others.

Principles of Ethics

Ethics deals with what is right or wrong, good or bad, fair or unfair, and responsible or irresponsible. It is concerned with both character and behavior. It discusses both governmental policy and more personal issues. On the one hand, it gets power from our social surroundings, established traditions, l4w, religion, and individual conscience. On the other hand, it critically evaluates each of these sources of strength. As a result, ethics is complicated, puzzling, and contentious. It resists simple, straightforward descriptions. Rights, duties, l4nguage use, what it means to live an ethical life, and how individuals make moral judgments are all topics covered in ethics. Moralizing may be thought of as an intellectual exercise, but it is more often an attempt to make sense of our gut feelings and responses. It is a subjective term, and many individuals have strong and obstinate opinions about what is good and wrong, which can put them at odd’s with the moral convictions of others. Despite the fact that morals differ from person to person, faith to religion, and culture to culture, many have been shown to be universal, deriving from fundamental human feelings.The moral code that drives a person's choices and behaviors throughout their life is represented by ethics. A moral code extends beyond the individual to encompass what is judged to be right and bad for a community or society as a whole.The decisions we make are central to ethics. We are continually faced with decisions that influence the quality of our life. We are conscious that the decisions we make have ramifications for both ourselves and others. We are mindful of our accountability for our conduct.
