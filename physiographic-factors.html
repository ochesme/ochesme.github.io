7. 2. What do you call the non-living factor in an ecosystem which describes the physical and chemical characteristics of the soil? * A. climatic factor B. edaphic factor C. physiographic factor D. soil factor​


EDAPHIC FACTOR

A non-living factor in an ecosystem which describes the physical and chemical characteristics of the soil is B. edaphic factor. Edaphic factor includes soil structure, temperature, pH, and salinity. A physical, biological, or chemical condition of the soil that has an impact on the creatures and processes that occur at edaphic factor.

Edaphic factor more definitions;

The edaphic factor is an abiotic factor that refers to the physical or chemical composition of a specific area's soil. The pH of the soil, the minerals in the soil, and the organic matter in the soil are all examples.

The soil qualities that affect the diversity of organisms living in the soil environment are known as edaphic factors. These include the following;

1. Soil structure

The size, shape, and arrangement of particles like sand, silt, and clay make up the soil structure. Micro-grained soils have been demonstrated to have more microbial biomass than coarse-grained soils. Bacterial development was observed to be favored by the lighter soil structure.

2. Salinity and pH of the soil

The pH of the soil is determined by the type of rock used to create it. Igneous rocks and sands combine to create acid soils. Carbonate rocks provide alkaline soils (e.g. limestone). Climate, rock weathering, organic matter, and human activities all have an impact on soil pH.

3. Soil temperature

Temperature is one of the most important edaphic elements in defining the limits of microbial development since groups of microorganisms grow at an optimal temperature and stop growing once they reach that temperature. The temperature of the soil influences not just microbial activity, but also seed sprouting, root growth, and nutrient availability.

4. Soil moisture

The water content of soil is characterized as soil moisture. It is one of the most crucial physical characteristics in agriculture since it has a direct impact on plant growth. Water is stored in the soil in a particular amount. Soil moisture levels change throughout time and space. In the case of arable land, it is dependent on soil qualities, vegetation type, evaporation intensity (therefore indirectly on temperature), rainfall volume and distribution, and irrigation.
