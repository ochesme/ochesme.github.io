13. the bimetallic strip of a thermostat consists of strips of copper and steel 10cm long that are fastened together and bent into a coil. If the strips have the same length at 20℃, what is the difference in their lengths at 200℃. ​


Answer:

The bimetallic strip in a thermostat consists of two different metals with different coefficients of thermal expansion. When heated or cooled, these metals expand or contract at different rates, causing the strip to bend.

To calculate the difference in length between the copper and steel strips at 200℃, we need to know their coefficients of thermal expansion. The coefficient of thermal expansion is a measure of how much a material expands or contracts when its temperature changes.

The coefficient of thermal expansion of copper is about 17 x 10^-6 /℃, and the coefficient of thermal expansion of steel is about 12 x 10^-6 /℃.

To calculate the difference in length, we can use the formula:

ΔL = L0 * α * ΔT

where ΔL is the change in length, L0 is the initial length of the strip (10cm in this case), α is the coefficient of thermal expansion, and ΔT is the change in temperature (180℃ in this case).

For copper, the change in length would be:

ΔL_copper = 10cm * 17 x 10^-6 /℃ * 180℃ = 30.6 µm

For steel, the change in length would be:

ΔL_steel = 10cm * 12 x 10^-6 /℃ * 180℃ = 21.6 µm

Therefore, the difference in length between the copper and steel strips at 200℃ would be:

ΔL = ΔL_copper - ΔL_steel = 9 µm

So the copper strip would be about 9 micrometers longer than the steel strip at 200℃.
