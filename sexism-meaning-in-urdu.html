10. what is Urdu relations to the Mongol empire​


Answer:

Urdu & Mongol Empire are not related other than a few distant connections. What is today known as Urdu was known as Hindustani before the 20th century, which evolved due to the fusion of dialect of Shauraseni Prakrit spoken in Delhi, Haryana & Western Uttar Pradesh, with Persian (Turkic influence is low) as it was the lingua franca of the Indian Islamic diaspora during the Mediaeval & Early Modern Age. This language was patronised by the Mughals, who were of Turko-Mongolic descent & claimed descent from Genghis Khan. That is the only connection between the two. Mongols never invaded the major part of India. They had control in Kashmir (Lohana dynasty being a Vassal) & temporarily held sway parts in parts of Western Punjab. Thus the influence of Mongol Culture or even Steppe Culture through Turks is minimal, as all these people whilst ruling India associated themselves with Persian Culture, albeit with influences of Indic cultures as well.

16. SEXISM. statement that show bias:​


Answer:

Sexism, prejudice or discrimination based on sex or gender, especially against women and girls. Although its origin is unclear, the term sexism emerged from the “second-wave” feminism of the 1960s through the ’80s and was most likely modeled on the civil rights movement’s term racism (prejudice or discrimination based on race). Sexism can be a belief that one sex is superior to or more valuable than another sex. It imposes limits on what men and boys can and should do and what women and girls can and should do. The concept of sexism was originally formulated to raise consciousness about the oppression of girls and women, although by the early 21st century it had sometimes been expanded to include the oppression of any sex, including men and boys, intersexual people, and transgender people.
