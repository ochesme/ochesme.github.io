17. How can you improve your table tennis footwork?


Answer:

Don't change your grip between forehand and backhand strokes.

Make sure you side shuffle into position between strokes.

Keep a closed racket angle throughout the drill.

Adjust the positioning of your feet based on your target.

Switch roles with your practice partner and control for them.

Explanation:

Answer Key ✨

Always move your outside foot whether you are moving to the left or right. Starting from your base position, to move from a backhand to a forehand, you move from your left foot to the right foot followed by your left then back to the right foot, creating short quick steps in a shuffling pattern from side to side.

21. ELearning Task 4. Based on the description and your understanding aboutdifferent footwork in table tennis, illustrate it in your notebookA. In and Out Footwork PatternB. Side to Side Footwork PatternC. Crossover Footwork Pattern​


A. In and Out Footwork Pattern:

When moving inwards towards the ball, step your right foot forward positioning your right leg under the table. After playing your shot, return back to the base position. Your left foot moves followed by the movement of your right foot under the table.

B. Side to Side Footwork Pattern:

So starting from the Ready Position, to move from the backhand to the forehand (for a right-hander), you move your left foot first, then your right foot, then your left foot again and then your right. So what you are doing is small, quick, side to side shuffling steps.

C. Crossover Footwork Pattern:

As the name suggests, your legs cross over each other for you to reach and catch the oncoming ball at a faster pace. To move from the backhand side to the wide forehand side, you cross your leg foot wide to the right side. Then using the left foot as a pivot, shift your weight to your left foot.
