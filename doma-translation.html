6. Stephen spent 3/4 hours doing his Math homework and 1/2 hours domas Sciencehomeword ton much more time did stephen spend on dorme ms Mathhomework than his Science home ork6. Asked7. Given:8. Operation:9. Number Sentence:10. Solution and Answers​


SUBTRACTION OF DISSIMILAR FRACTIONS

Problem:

Stephen spent 3/4 hours doing his Math homework and 1/2 hours doing his Science  homework. How much more time did Stephen spend on doing his Math  homework than his Science homework?

Asked:

The amount of time difference that Stephen spent doing his Math homework than Science homework.

Given:

The given are 3/4 hours of spending time in Math while 1/2 hours in doing Science.

Operation:

The operations that we will be using is subtraction. We will be subtracting two dissimilar fractions here.

Number Sentence:

The number sentence is: [tex]\frac{3}{4}-\frac{1}{2} = N[/tex]

where N refers to the time difference.

Solution and Answers​:

Since we cannot proceed to the direct subtraction of the numerators of the two fractions because they are dissimilar, we can use the cross-multiplication method or also known as the butterfly method. To do this, we need to multiply the numerator of 3/4 to the denominator of 1/2 then subtract it to the product of the numerator of 1/2 and denominator of 3/4 all over the product of the two denominators. Mathematically:

[tex]\frac{3}{4}-\frac{1}{2} = \frac{(3)(2)-(1)(4)}{(4)(2)}= \frac{6-4}{8} =\frac{2}{8} = \frac{1}{4}[/tex]

The answer is 2/8 which we transformed to its simplest form which is 1/4. Therefore, Stephen spent 1/4 hour more in his Math homework than in his Science homework.
