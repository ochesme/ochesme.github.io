19. how are the four forms of discourse significant to good writing? discuss​


Answer:

I'm cool guy you hehe how about you?

[tex]{\mathcal{ANSWER:}}[/tex]

There are four modes of discourse that you should be aware of. They are as follows:

•narration

•description

•exposition

•argument

NARRATION:

Narration is story telling. It involves relating a series of events, usually in a chronological order. The events narrated may be fictional (a made up story) or nonfictional ( the events really occurred). If the events actually happened, we give the writing another name such as biography, autobiography, history, after action report, or newspaper report.

DESCRPITION:

Description tells what things are like according to the five senses. A descriptive essay, or a descriptive passage in a story, tells how things look, sound, feel, taste, and smell. Nouns and adjectives can show what a person, place, or thing are like in their material aspects. But description often tries to do more than to enable readers to visualize characters, settings, and actions. It may also try to evoke a mood or atmosphere, and this is aided by the use of simile and metaphor.

EXPOSITION:

Exposition is the kind of writing that is used to inform. The prefix " Ex- " comes from Greek through Latin and means "out, or away from." The root of the word comes from the Latin verb "ponere" which means "to place." So translated literally, exposition means "to place out," and, of course, the thing that is placed out for us to see and understand is information. This mode of writing has several subtypes, the most common being process analysis, definition, classification and division, comparison and contrast, cause and effect, and problem and solution.

ARGUMENT:

The purpose of argument is to convince through logic. An argument is based on a belief or opinion that the writer holds as true. The statement of this opinion is called a "thesis." It is usually presented explicitly near the beginning of the argument. To convince readers that his opinion is true, the writer must build a case to support the thesis. Building a case requires presenting reasons for accepting the thesis, and then presenting evidence to support the reasons. If the reader accepts the reasons and the evidence, then he should agree with the thesis.

29. Discuss history as an academic discourse.​


History is used to study the past. History also helps humans understand and know what happened in the past. History clearly has its functions and uses for human life. In general, history serves as a source of knowledge and is used as a medium to trace facts and events that occurred in the past. This general function is intended so that humans in the next generation can take the events of the past as important lessons and experiences.

History as academic discourse.​

Because history is so important, in this article we will discuss what history is as a science. So, history can be said to be a science because it is a source of knowledge about past events. Events in the past were systematically arranged using scientific research methods, why use scientific studies? That's because history will affect the future, and it is very necessary to get the truth.

As a science, history is a science that has a major function in researching and investigating any events or events experienced by humans and society in the past. In conducting historical research, mastery of the scientific method is indispensable, it cannot be arbitrary in conducting historical research. This is because everything that is found later will become a science that determines conditions in the future.

History as academic discourse has the following characteristics:Empirical
Empirical is a word that comes from the Greek "empiro", which means human journey. All the experiences that happened later were recorded in relics and historical documents, which were then researched by historians in finding facts.Have an object
There is no science that does not have a goal, every science must have a clear goal and material object or target, all of which is to distinguish it from other sciences. While the objects of history are humans and society, the target is more emphasized on humans from the point of view of time.Have a theory
The theory is an opinion that has been put forward as a description of an event. Theory in history contains a collection of the basic principles of science.Have a method
In conducting historical research, it has its own method of using observations accompanied by evidence to make conclusions about history as a science, studying throughout human life.Have generalizations
