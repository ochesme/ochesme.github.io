Answer:

40

Step-by-step explanation:

This is a form of a system of equations. Let us simplify the equations to join like terms.

2 kiwis + 1 apple = 22 ... (1)

2 pears + 1 kiwi = 28 ... (2)

3 apples + 2 pears = 38 ... (3)

2 apples + 1 kiwi + 2 pears = ? ... (4)

Let us try to isolate one of the variables in order to get the others.

2[(3) - (2)] + (1)

3 apples + 2 pears = 38

-1 kiwi      - 2 pears = -28

3 apples -  1 kiwi     = 10

6 apples - 2 kiwis = 20

1 apple + 2 kiwis   = 22

7 apples = 42

1 apple = 6

From this, we can already get the answer for the fourth equation.

2 apples + (1 kiwi + 2 pears)

= 2 (6) + 28

= 12 + 28

= 40

However, if you would like to know the value of the pear and kiwi, just substitute back to the equation.
