9. what is -simple pastwhat is-simple futurewhat is-for present perpectwhat is-past perpectwhat is-puture perpect tencePLS HELP ME​


Answer:

Simple Past–

The simple past is a verb tense that is used to talk about things that happened or existed before now.The simple past tense shows that you are talking about something that has already happened. Unlike the past continuous tense, which is used to talk about past events that happened over a period of time, the simple past tense emphasizes that the action is finished.

Simple Future–The simple future is a verb tense that’s used to talk about things that haven’t happened yet.

Present Perfect– The present perfect tense refers to an action or state that either occurred at an indefinite time in the past (e.g., we have talked before) or began in the past and continued to the present time (e.g., he has grown impatient over the last hour). This tense is formed by have/has + the past participle.

Past Perfect–The past perfect, also called the pluperfect, is a verb tense used to talk about actions that were completed before some point in the past.

Future perfect tense–The future perfect tense is for talking about an action that will be completed between now and some point in the future.
