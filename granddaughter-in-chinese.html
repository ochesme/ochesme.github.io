2. A 60-kg grandfather and his 20-kg granddaughter are balanced on a seesaw. If the granddaughter is sitting 3 meters from the pivot point, the grandfather must be sitting ____ from it​


Answer:

4 meters

Explanation:

To balance the seesaw, the grandfather must be sitting farther from the pivot point than the granddaughter. This is because the granddaughter weighs less than the grandfather and therefore creates less torque (a measure of rotational force) on the seesaw.

To calculate how far the grandfather must be sitting from the pivot point, we can use the principle of torque.

Torque = Force x Distance

The force in this case is the weight of the grandfather and granddaughter, and the distance is the distance from the pivot point.

Torque = (60 kg + 20 kg) x 3 m

Torque = 80 kg x 3 m

To balance this torque, the grandfather must be sitting farther from the pivot point. We can use the following formula to calculate it:

Torque = (60 kg) x distance

distance = Torque / (60 kg)

distance = (80 kg x 3m) / (60 kg) = 4 m

Therefore, if the granddaughter is sitting 3 meters from the pivot point, the grandfather must be sitting 4 meters from it.

7. Lola Cely divided her farm to her granddaughters in the ratio of 6:4:2. If the total area of the farm is 240 hectares, what is the area of each one?


Answer:

120:80:40:

Step-by-step explanation:

Given: 240 hectares in total

Ratio: 6:4:2

To determine the answer, simply add the ratio 6, 4, and 2 which is 12.

Divide 240 hectares to 12 which is 20.

Then, multiply 20 to the given ratio.

20x6= 120, 20x4= 80, 20x2= 40

To check, simply add the answer 120, 80, 40 which is 240. Equal to the given.

[tex]\large{\mathcal{QUESTION:}}[/tex]

Lola Cely divided her farm to her granddaughters in the ratio of 6:4:2. If the total area of the farm is 240 hectares, what is the area of the third grand daughter?

[tex] \\ [/tex]

[tex]\large{\mathcal{SOLUTION:}}[/tex]

Let x be the constant to the proportion:

[tex] \\ [/tex]

Solving for the constant of the proportion:

6x+4x+2x = 24012x = 24012x/12 = 240/12 x = 20

Now that we found the constant , we will substitute it to the ratios:

[tex] \\ [/tex]

First daughter = 6x = 6(20) = 120

Second daughter = 4x = 4(20) = 80

Third daughter = 2x = 2(20) = 40

[tex] \\ [/tex]

Henceforth, the area of the third grand daughter is 40 hectares.

[tex] \\ [/tex]

[tex]\large{\mathcal{ANSWER:}}[/tex]
