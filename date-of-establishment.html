6. are the principles to establish relative dates enough to prove the earth geological past? explain​


Answer:

Law of Superposition

Relative age means age in comparison with other rocks, either younger or older. The relative ages of rocks are important for understanding Earth's history. New rock layers are always deposited on top of existing rock layers. Therefore, deeper layers must be older than layers closer to the surface

15. Are the principles used to establish relative dates enough to prove the Earth'sgeological past? Explain.​


Answer:

The principle of faunal succession is based on the appearance of fossils in sedimentary rocks. As organisms exist at the same time period throughout the world, their presence or (sometimes) absence may be used to provide a relative age of the formations in which they are found.
