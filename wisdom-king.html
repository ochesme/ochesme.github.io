Solomon is the second child of David and Bathsheba, the widow of Uriah the Hittite. He is the biblical king famous because of his wisdom. He was the author of several books in the Bible which includes Proverbs, Ecclesiastes and Song of Solomon. The Wisdom of Solomon, which is notably known as Book of Wisdom is a book about wisdom.

The best known story where his wisdom was tested is his judgment of Solomon. It is a story about a two women who each bore their child. One of the children died. The woman which is the mother of the child that died is claiming the child of the other woman as hers. To be fair, Solomon concluded that the child will be cut in half since both are claiming that they are the mother.

However, out of the real mother's love and compassion, she sacrificed herself and ask that her child be given to the other claimant. Solomon’s wisdom determined the real mother, so he gave the child to the real mother - the one who doesn't want the baby to be cut into half.

Accounts from the Bible Regarding King Solomon’s Wisdom: 1 Kings 3: 16 – 28 1 Kings 4: 29 1 Kings 5: 12 2 Chronicles 1: 7 – 11

In the First book of Kings Chapter 3 verses 16 to 28, it was narrated that King Solomon made use of his wisdom in deciding which of the two harlots who have delivered a child should be given its custody. Since both women are claiming that the living child was theirs so he asked for a sword and act as if he will divide the child into two. The one who begs King Solomon to spare the child and give it to the other had received the king’s approval so she was able to keep the child.

On the same book of Kings chapter 4, 29th verse, tells that King Solomon’s wisdom excels the wisdom of all the wisest men of the East, even the wisdom of Egypt.  

In 1 Kings Chapter 5 verse 12, it says that King Solomon’s wisdom caused him to be at peace with the man named Hiram. Hiram is the king of Tyre who had extended help with King Solomon in building his temple from choosing and gathering the timbers.

Verses 7 – 11 of the First Book of First Chronicles tell the story of how King Solomon had gained his wisdom. It was an understanding heart that he had asked from God which eventually gives him the wisdom.
