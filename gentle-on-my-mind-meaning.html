16. What does do not go gentle into that good night mean?


Do Not Go Gentle Into That Good Night

The poem is written by the poet Dylan Thomas and it is about the inevitability of man's death. The dying of light or the good night refers to man's descent into old age and death. Dylan Tomas urges other men to resist the urge to die.

How is it possible to resist death as a man?

There is a saying that some men die at 25 and only get buried at 75. Dylan Thomas may not be really referring to the physical death but the death of dreams, ambition, and spirit of man.

Some people give up early or at the middle of life. While others wilt at the helm of bad news, others at the threat of failure. There are others who simply cannot be bothered. If man does not fight for his right to live and his right to be happy, can he truly say that he lived?

He urges man to live a life without regret and do what they can to live fully. When old age or frailty comes, man will become self-aware of his own mortality and will come to remember the times when he should have done more or done differently.
