1. what is onsite classes?​


Answer:

Onsite classes refer to classes that are conducted in a physical location, such as a classroom, rather than online or remotely. Students attend the classes in person and receive instruction from a teacher or instructor who is present in the same location.

Onsite classes are the traditional form of teaching and learning, and they offer several advantages over online classes, such as the ability to interact face-to-face with the teacher and other students, access to hands-on learning experiences, and a structured and disciplined environment for learning.

Onsite classes are commonly offered in schools, colleges, and universities, as well as in vocational training centers and other educational institutions. They are typically scheduled at fixed times and follow a set curriculum or syllabus.

However, due to the COVID-19 pandemic, many educational institutions have had to shift to online classes to ensure the safety of students and teachers. As the pandemic subsides, it is expected that onsite classes will gradually resume, although many institutions may continue to offer a mix of online and onsite classes to provide greater flexibility and accessibility to students.>-<

Students are physically present in the classroom and can interact with the teacher and their peers in real-time.
