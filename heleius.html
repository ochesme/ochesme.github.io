1. helium gas has a volume of 250 ml at 0 Degrees celcius at 1.0 atm. What will be the final pressure if the volume is reduced to 100 ml at 45 degrees celsius


Given Data:

V1       = 250 L                                                 V2 = 100L

T1 (kelvin)= 0 + 273.15 = 273.15K                   T2 = 45 + 273.15 = 318.15K  

P1 = 1 atm                                                          P2 = unknown ?

Note: kelvin temperature will used in the computation which is

K=degree celsius + 273.15

Solution:

In this problem we can used the formula of combined gas law which is  

V1P1/T1 = V2P2/T2

To simplify we can used the formula

P2 = V1×P1×T2 / T1×V2

P2 = 250L × 1 atm × 318.15K / 273.15K × 100L

P2 =  79, 537.5 atm / 27, 315

P2 = 2.912 atm

The final pressure is 2.912 atm

note: make sure to cancel the unit of Kelvin and Liter

Helium gas has a volume of 250 ml at 0 Degrees celcius at 1.0 atm. What will be the final pressure if the volume is reduced to 100 ml at 45 degrees celsius

We have the following information:  

V1 (initial volume) = 250 mL → 0.25 L

V2 (final volume) = 100 mL → 0.1 L

T1 (initial temperature) = 0ºC (in Kelvin)

TK = TC + 273.15 → TK = 0 + 273.15 → TK = 273.15

T1 (initial temperature) = 273.15 K

T2 (final temperature) = 45º C (in Kelvin)

TK = TC + 273.15 → TK = 45 + 273.15 → TK = 318.15

T2 (final temperature) = 318.15 K

P1 (initial pressure) = 1 atm

P2 (final pressure) = ? (in atm)

Now, we apply the data of the variables above to the General Equation of Gases, let's see:

[tex]\dfrac{P_1*V_1}{T_1} =\dfrac{P_2*V_2}{T_2}[/tex]

[tex]\dfrac{1*0.25}{273.15} =\dfrac{P_2*0.1}{318.15}[/tex]

[tex]\dfrac{0.25}{273.15} =\dfrac{0.1\:P_2}{318.15}[/tex]

multiply the means by the extremes

[tex]273.15*0.1\:P_2 = 0.25*318.15[/tex]

[tex]27.315\:P_2 = 79.5375[/tex]

[tex]P_2 = \dfrac{79.5375}{27.315}[/tex]

[tex]P_2 = 2.911861614...[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\boxed{P_2 \approx 2.912\:atm}}\end{array}}\qquad\checkmark[/tex]

Answer:

The final pressure is approximately 2.912 atm

8. a 2,000 ml sample of helium at 300 degrees celsius and 700 torr is compressed to a volume of 850 ml at 150 degrees celsius what is the new gas​


Given:

[tex]P_{1} = \text{700 torr}[/tex]

[tex]T_{1} = \text{300°C + 273 = 573 K}[/tex]

[tex]V_{1} = \text{2000 mL}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = \text{850 mL}[/tex]

[tex]T_{2} = \text{150°C + 273 = 423 K}[/tex]

Unknown:

[tex]P_{2}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]\frac{P_{1}V_{1}}{T_{1}} = \frac{P_{2}V_{2}}{T_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]P_{2} = P_{1} × \frac{V_{1}}{V_{2}} × \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}[/tex]

[tex]P_{2} = \text{700 torr} × \frac{\text{2000 mL}}{\text{850 mL}} × \frac{\text{423 K}}{\text{573 K}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{P_{2} = \text{1216 torr}}[/tex]

9. when 500ml of helium gas at 25 degrees Celsius is cooled at constant pressure to 10k. calculate the volume of gas​


Given:

[tex]V_{1} = \text{500 mL}[/tex]

[tex]T_{1} = \text{25°C + 273 = 298 K}[/tex]

[tex]T_{2} = \text{10 K}[/tex]

Unknown:

[tex]V_{2}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]\frac{V_{1}}{T_{1}} = \frac{V_{2}}{T_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = V_{1} × \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = \text{500 mL} × \frac{\text{10 K}}{\text{298 K}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{V_{2} = \text{16.78 mL}}[/tex]
