Answer:

1.A. 0.53 V

To calculate the voltage generated between the region in the wingtips of an aircraft flying horizontally, we can use the formula:

Voltage (V) = Magnetic field strength (B) * Velocity (v) * Length of wing (l)

Plugging in the given values:

B = 3.8 * 10^-5 T (given)

v = 220 m/s (given)

l = 27 m (given)

V = (3.8 * 10^-5 T) * (220 m/s) * (27 m) = 0.53 V

2.C. 5.7 * 10^15 J

The energy emitted by visible light is quantized in discrete packets called photons. The energy of a photon can be calculated using the formula:

Energy (E) = Planck's constant (h) * Frequency (f)

The frequency of light can be calculated using the formula:

Frequency (f) = Speed of light (c) / Wavelength (λ)

Plugging in the given values:

Power (P) = 100 W (given)

Wavelength (λ) = 5.3 * 10^-7 m (given)

Speed of light (c) = 3 * 10^8 m/s (constant)

Planck's constant (h) = 6.63 * 10^-34 J·s (constant)

f = (3 * 10^8 m/s) / (5.3 * 10^-7 m) ≈ 5.66 * 10^14 Hz

E = (6.63 * 10^-34 J·s) * (5.66 * 10^14 Hz) ≈ 5.7 * 10^15 J

3.B. The Himalayan Mountains formed at the convergence of different plates.

The observation of fossils from Mt. Everest in the Himalayan Mountains, including extinct marine organisms, suggests that the Himalayan Mountains formed at the convergence of different tectonic plates. The collision of these plates would have caused the uplift and deformation of the rocks, leading to the formation of the Himalayas, which are known to be a result of the ongoing collision between the Indian and Eurasian plates.
