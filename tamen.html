2. 14-15: 2 Parts of $tamen *​


Parts of Stamen

Stamen: The filament and anther are the two elements of the stamen. Pollen is produced by the anther (male reproductive cells). The filament is what keeps the anther in place. Pollen falls on the stigma during fertilization, and a tube extends down the style and into the ovary.

The male reproductive part of a flower is the stamen. The stamen is made up of a long, slender stalk, the filament, and a two-lobed anther at the apex in all but a few surviving angiosperms. The anther is made up of four sac-like structures (microsporangia) that generate pollen.

The stamen's primary job is to create pollen grains, which contain the male gametes, or sex cells, required for reproduction. These gametes are produced in the anther, which is found within the stamen.
