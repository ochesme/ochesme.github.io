3. Curing meat aims to___________for future use and add desirable flavor and color to the meat.weight, convert, bacteria, evisceration, preservatives, consistency, sanitation, poultry, salt, preserve, tools, equipment​


preserve

Explanation:

Cured meat is meat that has been preserved through ageing, drying, canning, salting, brining or smoking. The goal of curing is to slow spoilage and prevent the growth of microorganisms. Curing dates back to ancient times, when it was essential for storing meat safely and preventing food poisoning.

Curing is the addition to meats of some combination of salt, sugar, nitrite and/or nitrate for the purposes of preservation, flavor and color. Some publications distinguish the use of salt alone as salting, corning or salt curingand reserve the word curing for the use of salt with nitrates/nitrites.

14. composition and nutritive value of fish(scaling, cutting of tails and fins, eviscerating, cleansing, canning)​


Answer:

Fish is a nutritious food that is high in protein, omega-3 fatty acids, and minerals such as iodine and zinc. The composition and nutritive value of fish can be affected by various processing methods, including scaling, cutting of tails and fins, eviscerating, cleansing, and canning.

Scaling: Scaling is the process of removing the scales from the fish's skin. Scaling does not affect the nutritive value of the fish, but it may affect the texture and appearance of the final product.

Cutting of tails and fins: This process involves removing the tail and fins of the fish. This step has no significant effect on the nutritional value of the fish, but it can make the final product more visually appealing.

Eviscerating: Eviscerating is the process of removing the internal organs of the fish. This step can affect the nutritional value of the fish by removing some of the fat and the liver, which is a good source of vitamins A and D.

Cleansing: Cleansing is the process of cleaning the fish to remove any dirt or debris. This step has no significant effect on the nutritional value of the fish, but it can improve the overall appearance and quality of the final product.

Canning: Canning is the process of preserving fish by sealing it in a can. This step can affect the nutritional value of the fish by reducing the levels of certain vitamins such as vitamin C and B. However, canning can be beneficial in preserving fish for longer periods of time and making it more accessible to people in different regions.

It's worth noting that the composition and nutritive value of fish can vary depending on the species and the fishing method used, and the nutritional value can also be affected by the way it is cooked

23. The poultry is immersed in hot water to pull the feathers easier.. Select one: O a. plucking O b. picking O c. waxing O d. eviscerating O e. slaughtering O f. deboning O g. food spoilage Oh. cross contamination Oi. dry picking O j. scalding


The poultry is immersed in hot water to pull the feathers easier. This step called scalding.

Poultry Post Cutting-Process

1. Hot water immersion (scalding)

The chicken is dipped in hot water after the chicken is slaughtered to make it easier to remove the feathers. The immersion time and the dyeing water temperature depend on the condition of the chicken. that the immersion process in warm water depends on the age and condition of the poultry.

2. Removal of feathers (defeathering)

hair removal can be done using two types, namely using a machine (plucker) and manually by hand.

3. Carcass preparation and cutting (castling)

The last stage is cutting the carcass, washing and polluting the carcass. Carcass washing uses water at a temperature of 5-10°C with a chlorine content of 0.5-1 ppm, this is to avoid and suppress bacterial growth, so that the quality and safety of chicken carcass is maintained.

5. Packaging and labeling of products (packaging and labeling).

Packaging aims to protect the carcass from damage that is too fast, both physical damage, chemical changes, and contamination of microorganisms, as well as to display the product in an attractive way.

6. Product cooling and storage

A good chicken carcass cooling technique uses a room with a temperature of 4-5°C with a cooling time of 15-20 minutes and no more than 8 hours after slaughter, so that the physical, chemical and microbiological conditions of the chicken carcass remain good.

Frozen storage aims to maintain and protect carcasses from various harmful contaminants, physical quality can be maintained, nutritional quality remains good and can suppress bacterial growth, so as to extend shelf life 1-3 months. The maximum temperature in the frozen storage room is -20°C.
