27. Examine the stylistic devices Nikolai Gogol uses to examine themes in his play the Government Inspector.​


Answer:

Nikolai Gogol's play "The Government Inspector" is a satirical work that criticizes the corrupt and inefficient bureaucracy of the Russian government during the 19th century. Gogol employs a variety of stylistic devices to examine the play's themes, including irony, hyperbole, caricature, and absurdity. Here are some examples:

Irony: Gogol employs irony throughout the play to highlight the gap between appearance and reality. The title character is expected to be a highly competent and respected government official, but in reality, he is a fraud who is only interested in taking advantage of the corrupt officials he encounters. Gogol also uses verbal irony in the dialogue between the characters, as they say one thing while meaning another.

Hyperbole: Gogol uses exaggeration to emphasize the ridiculousness of the government officials and their actions. For example, the town mayor and other officials are shown to be so corrupt that they are willing to offer bribes to the supposed government inspector in exchange for a favorable report.

Caricature: Gogol uses caricature to create larger-than-life versions of his characters, which exaggerate their physical and personality traits for comedic effect. The town mayor is depicted as a bumbling, self-important official who is more concerned with his own reputation than the welfare of the town.

Absurdity: Gogol creates absurd situations and characters to satirize the absurdity of the government bureaucracy. For example, the officials are so incompetent that they mistake a lowly clerk for the government inspector and treat him with the utmost respect, despite his obvious lack of qualifications.

Overall, Gogol's use of these stylistic devices helps to highlight the themes of corruption, incompetence, and absurdity in the Russian government during the 19th century. By using humor and satire to critique the government, Gogol is able to convey his message to the audience in a more engaging and memorable way.
