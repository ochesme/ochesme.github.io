7. What is the meaning of the ode to a dead millionaire by pablo neruda?


Ode To A Dead Millionaire

This poem is written by Pablo Neruda. Written supposedly as a tribute to a millionaire who had passed away but never been glorified during his lifetime due to his greediness. It is an expression of Neruda's dismay for this man. The man who had lived like a God but never deserve to be praised.

Stanza Analysis of the Poem

On the first stanza, Neruda points out the millionaire's love for power and money. He said that the greatness of this man is covered with greed. He said that even if the man is a millionaire his money cannot prolong his life.

On the second stanza, Neruda shows some hope by saying that tyranny have died and that there was happiness since the abusive power of the millionaire has merely ended.

On the last stanza, Neruda expounds his feelings of dismay for this man. He said that it will be pointless to tell him anything for he will no longer be present to hear them and nothing will ever change. He even say that even death do not compensate what this man have caused and all the damages he had brought.

8. What is the meaning of the poem, Dead water by Wen Yi Tuo?​


Answer:

The creator of this poem was Wen yi-tuo, he is a Chinese and he was born September 15, 1946. He studied literature at United States of America. The Dead Water was published 1925 when he traveled back to china and he took a University teaching.

The Dead Water is all about our environment.This poetry was influenced by western models.During 1925 he joined the Cresent moon society and wrote essays. It has a A-B-C-B rhyme structure which is difficult to translate into English. This poem is an example of yi-tuo early political learnings, interpreting it as a bitter satire on the state of the government. The Dead Water is the poem employs a complicated rhythm structure of varying syllable length units. This poem was a interesting to explore, it is the idea of environmentalism. The second stanza the speaker states that the ditch could be redeemed by emerald grass and peach blossoms, but left to its own devices it only becomes a feeding area for the “gnats”who could be those who wish to feel upon the earth. The dead water will croak its song of delight which could be the tendencies of human towards self-destruction.

In this poem yi-tuo seems to be suggesting that there might be some beauty in ugliness, and asking if all dead things are truly hopeless. Because of the speaker attention to the detail the poem provides a useful overview of the topic the dead water.

20. Explain the meaning of: "As the body without the spirit is dead, so faith without deeds is dead."


Answer:

James 2:14–26 makes the case that how one acts—their ''works''—are a sign of the kind of ''faith'' they possess. So-called-''faith'' which doesn't lead a person to participate in good works is not a saving faith; it is a dead thing. It is pointless and meaningless to believe, or ''wish,'' a poverty-stricken person to be well, if such an opinion leads to no action. In exactly the same way, James insists that it is not enough to mentally agree about certain facts of God. If what a person believes about God does not lead them to act accordingly, then their ''faith'' is not saving faith. It is merely opinion. James never says that faith is not essential for salvation. He never claims works are required to obtain or keep salvation. He is, however, crystal clear that truly saving faith cannot be separated from the evidence of good works.

28. what is the purpose of grass if the horse is dead mean 4 sentence


The "What is the purpose of grass if the horse is dead" is a well known idiom in Tagalog known as "Aanihin mo ang damo kung patay na ang kabayo".

The best way to explain this idiom is in a story. There was a family who desperately need financial help, so they seek and begged their neighbor because a member of their family is in critical condition. Unfortunately, their neighbor refused to give them assistance. The person died and the family whom refused to help gave assistance or "limos", flowers, and snacks for the funeral.
