6. Every English setter likes to hunt. Duke is an English setter, so Duke likes to hunt is inductive or deductive?


The state that claims that “Every English Setter likes to hunt. Duke is an English Setter, so Duke likes to hunt” is a product of Deductive Reasoning. This is because it is followed by two premises.

Premise #1 - Every English Setter likes to hunt. Premise #2 - Duke is an English Setter, Conclusion - So Duke likes to hunt.

Under deductive reasoning, the conclusion was made after providing the 2 premises that support it.

22. Describe your professional field as mold setter?​


The Mold Setter sets up and adjusts automatic compression, injection, or transfer machines used to mold plastic materials to specified shapes, and utilizes knowledge of machine functions.

A mold setter positions molds used to cast products and parts. People in this job perform the positioning and placement of the mold either by hand or using a machine. A mold setter may work with a variety of different materials, including plastic, metals, lead, and composites.

Mold setters are skilled tradespeople specialized in setting molds for the manufacturing of products or components made of various materials. They are responsible for mixing materials, positioning and aligning molds, and overseeing temperatures and timing necessary for materials to solidify.

Alternate titles: Newton’s first law

law of inertia, also called Newton’s first law, postulate in physics that, if a body is at rest or moving at a constant speed in a straight line, it will remain at rest or keep moving in a straight line at constant speed unless it is acted upon by a force. The law of inertia was first formulated by Galileo Galilei for horizontal motion on Earth and was later generalized by René Descartes. Before Galileo it had been thought that all horizontal motion required a direct cause, but Galileo deduced from his experiments that a body in motion would remain in motion unless a force (such as friction) caused it to come. This law is also the first of Isaac Newton’s three laws of motion.
