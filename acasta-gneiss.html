28. ___________ is a nonfoliated metamorphic rock A. gneiss B. marble C. phyllite D. schist


Answer:

B. marble

Explanation:

Because nonfoliated metamorphic rocks frequently lack platy minerals like micas, they lack foliated texture. They are frequently caused by touch or regional metamorphism. Marble, quartzite, greenstone, hornfelt, and anthracite are among examples.


29. is there achance that shale minerals can form into gneiss​


Answer:

Gneiss can form in a variety of ways. Shale, a sedimentary rock, is the most typical starting point. Shale can be metamorphosed into slate, phyllite, schist, and lastly gneiss by regional metamorphism.

Clay particles in shale turn into micas and grow in size throughout this process. The platy micas eventually recrystallize into granular crystals. The transition into gneiss is marked by the presence of granular materials.

Granite can also transform into a banded rock known as "granite gneiss" when subjected to extreme heat and pressure. This transformation is typically more of a structural than a mineralogical alteration.
