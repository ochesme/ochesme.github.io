20. What weight of NaCl in 800g acetone is needed to bring boiling point to 60°C? (Boiling point of pure acetone= 55.95°C ; Kb of acetone = 1.71 °C/m)​


Answer:

Explanation:

Given:

Mass of acetone (solvent) = 800 g

Boiling point of pure acetone = 55.95°C

Desired boiling point = 60°C

Kb of acetone = 1.71°C/m

To find:

Weight of NaCl needed

Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the change in boiling point

ΔTb = Tb(solution) - Tb(solvent)

ΔTb = 60°C - 55.95°C

ΔTb = 4.05°C

Step 2: Calculate the molality of the solution

Kb = ΔTb / m

m = ΔTb / Kb

m = 4.05°C / 1.71°C/m

m = 2.37 m

Step 3: Calculate the moles of acetone

Molar mass of acetone (CH3COCH3) = 58.08 g/mol

moles of acetone = mass / molar mass

moles of acetone = 800 g / 58.08 g/mol

moles of acetone = 13.77 mol

Step 4: Calculate the moles of solute needed

moles of solute = molality x kg solvent

moles of solute = 2.37 mol/kg x 0.8 kg

moles of solute = 1.896 mol

Step 5: Calculate the weight of NaCl needed

Molar mass of NaCl = 58.44 g/mol

weight of NaCl = moles of solute x molar mass

weight of NaCl = 1.896 mol x 58.44 g/mol

weight of NaCl = 110.9 g

Answer:

110.9 g of NaCl is needed to bring the boiling point of 800g acetone to 60°C.

Explanation:

The 4 nitrogenous base compounds that make up DNA are adenine (A), thymine (T), guanine (G), cytosine (C). Adenine (A) pairs with thymine (T) grouped in purine bases. Meanwhile, guanine (G) pairs with cytosine (C) grouped in pyrimidine bases.

The following is a further explanation.

Discussion:

Understanding DNA

DNA (Deoxribonucleic Acid) or Deoxyribose Nucleic acid is an acid that is present in all living things. This DNA becomes a kind of genetic code which will determine the shape of the organism.

The main role of the DNA molecule is the long-term storage of information. DNA is often compared to a set of blueprints or recipes, or codes, because it contains the instructions needed to build other components of the cell, such as proteins and RNA molecules. The segments of DNA that carry this genetic information are called genes, but other DNA sequences that have a structural purpose, or are involved in regulating the use of genetic information.

DNA can replicate i.e. form a copy of itself. Each DNA strand contains a specific base sequence. Each base is also linked by sugar and phosphate molecules. If the base forms a rung (horizontal), then the sugar and phosphate molecules form the vertical part of the ladder.

DNA properties

The properties of DNA include:

• Is chromosomal material as a carrier of genetic information, through the activity of cell division.

• Constant amount of DNA in each cell type and species. Constant in the sense of being fixed and unchanging. For example, DNA in cats is different from the amount of DNA in other mammals. Likewise, the amount of DNA in humans and primates has different instructions.

• DNA content in cells depends on the nature of the ploidy (genome) of the cell or the number of chromosomes in the cell.

Can do replication, namely forming derivatives or duplicating oneself. The replicated DNA (daughter DNA) has an identical base sequence to that of the parental double helix (parent DNA).

• In the cells of prokaryotic organisms (bacteria), DNA is single-chain. In eukaryotic cells, DNA is a double helix.

• At boiling point temperatures or at extreme pHs (less than 3 or more than 10), DNA denatures (opens). If the environment is returned to normal, DNA can again form the double helix.

DNA function

1. DNA functions as a cell inheritance

DNA or deoxyribonucleic acid is a material that can be inherited in all cells. DNA precisely replicates (reproduces itself) during each generation of cells.

When the cell divides, identical DNA from the parent is shared with each daughter cell. Thus, DNA provides color for all future generations of single cells and total multicellular organisms.

2. DNA functions in controlling cell activity

DNA in controlling the activity is carried out by determining the synthesis of enzymes and other proteins. Proteins serve as catalysts and construction of metabolic reactions, provide the raw materials for building structures, support movement, the environment and other cells, and control cell growth and division.

3. DNA functions as a collection of units of information

Genes, which are functional fragments of fragments in DNA, function in determining the amino acid sequence of a protein.

DNA structure

In general, the structural features of DNA are double helix (double helix); composed of nitrogenous bases Adenine, Guanine, Thymine and Cytosine; and is a polymer of nucleotide monomers (phosphate-sugar deoxyribose-base nitrogen).

The structure of DNA is a double helix composed of two polynucleotide strands connected to each other by weak hydrogen bonds. The hydrogen bond is formed between two nitrogenous bases, purine and pyrimidine, which are paired with each other. Adenine (purine base) pairs with Thymine (pyrimidine base) which is connected by a double bond, while Guanine (purine base) is paired with Cytosine (pyrimidine base) which is connected by a triple bond.

The nitrogenous base is connected to a deoxyribose sugar in the back DNA chain. Deoxyribose sugar is a modification of ribose sugar, which is a sugar with 5 carbon atoms, where the number 2 carbon atom loses its oxygen atom. Therefore, this sugar is called deoxy which means loss of oxygen.

28. What is abstinence, and why is abstinence the only certain way to prevent STIs?


What is abstinence? and why is abstinence the only certain way to prevent STIs or sexually transmitted infections?

Abstinence is considered the most effective way of preventing someone to acquire STIs such as the human immunodeficiency virus (HIV) that results in acquired immunodeficiency syndrome (AIDS). With this practice, the risk of getting STIs is virtually zero, especially when an individual chooses to have a complete sexual abstinence, which is one of the two types of abstinence, the other one is partial sexual abstinence. Abstinence’s effectiveness is technically almost 100 percent. However, there remains the likelihood of being infected, not by sexual intercourse, but through other vehicles such as infected needles. Apart from abstinence, both sexes can practice refusal skills to prevent and control STIs and HIV/AIDS. One way is to say "No" to sex or unprotected sex in variety of ways, how to insist on the use of condoms or other methods of contraception, how to use body language that reinforced the verbal message, how to repeatedly refuse sex or insist on condom use, how to suggest alternative activities, and how to help build the relationship while refusing unprotected sex or refusing to have sex at all.  

Apart from the aforementioned refusal skills, one should also follow the guidelines below:

Avoid use of alcohol and other drugs Choose friends that practice abstinence (for further abstinence definition, you should click this link: https://brainly.ph/question/785645) Do not engage in sexual activities with more than one person Do not engage in sexual activities with a high-risk individual Do not engage in unprotected sex

Furthermore, several governments, including the Philippines, have implemented campaigns and enacted certain laws to protect those who are infected and to prevent the further spread of HIV and other STIs. In the Philippines, the Republic Act No. 8504 or the Philippine AIDS Prevention and Control Act of 1998 was passed by the Congress on July 28, 1997. The Act promulgates “policies and prescribing measures for the prevention and control of HIV/AIDS in the Philippines, instituting a nationwide HIV/AIDS information and educational program, establishing a comprehensive HIV/AIDS monitoring system, strengthening the Philippine National AIDS Council, and for other purposes” (PNAC). The Act also provides for education and information dissemination in various institutions and agencies, safe practice and procedures (testing, screening, and counseling) in health organizations and associations, monitoring of cases with confidentiality, and programs for anti-discriminatory acts (in the workplace, hospitals, and other public institutions).
